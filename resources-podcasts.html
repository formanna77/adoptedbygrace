Podcasts & Media

Trusted audio and video resources for learning biblical theology on the go — podcasts, YouTube channels, and online courses from the finest Reformed teachers and theologians.

"Faith comes from hearing, and hearing through the word of Christ." — Romans 10:17 (ESV)

Start Here

New to audio Reformed theology? These 5 episodes are the perfect entry point. They introduce the big ideas clearly, warmly, and compellingly — what you need to understand biblical sovereignty and how it changes everything.

Chosen by God (Opening Lectures)
Ligonier Ministries R.C. Sproul
Sproul's landmark series opening — the clearest explanation of unconditional election you'll hear. Shows why this doctrine is not only biblical but pastoral and beautiful.
Why start here: If you watch nothing else, watch this first. It answers every major objection in under 30 minutes.
Does God Choose Who Believes?
Ask Pastor John John Piper
Piper's direct, pastoral answer to the question that stops most people: If God chooses, why do we choose? He shows how both are true in ways that will blow your mind.
Why start here: Gets straight to the biggest objection and resolves it with warmth and Scripture.
God's Sovereignty & Prayer
Renewing Your Mind R.C. Sproul
The answer to the pastoral objection: If God is sovereign, why pray? Sproul shows why election makes prayer not less important but more — this is the episode that changes how people pray.
Why start here: Bridges doctrine to life. Shows that sovereignty doesn't drain meaning from your choices or prayers.
The Five Points of Calvinism Explained Simply
Truth for Life Alistair Begg
Begg walks through TULIP with warmth and accessibility — no intimidating jargon. For those who hear "TULIP" and panic, this episode makes it all make sense.
Why start here: Accessible and warm. Perfect if Reformed terminology feels foreign to you.
Divine Predestination in Scripture
The Dividing Line James White
White goes deep into Scripture — Romans 9, Ephesians 1, Acts 4:28 — showing the biblical case for predestination. Rigorous but clear. For those who want the Scripture foundation.
Why start here: Want the biblical proof? This is it. Scholarly but not dense.
Why Would God Predestine Anyone?
Mortification of Spin Alliance of Confessing Evangelicals
A witty, thoughtful Reformed discussion on the "why" behind predestination. Shows that election is not arbitrary but rooted in God's character and glory. Clever theology that's actually fun.
Why start here: Combines intelligence, humor, and heart. Theology doesn't have to be dry — this proves it.
