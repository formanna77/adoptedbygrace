# Seasonal Content Calendar — Adopted by Grace

A 12-month guide mapping key dates to existing site content, newsletter themes, and social strategies.

---

## APRIL 2026

### Good Friday — April 18
**Theme:** The cost of redemption. Christ's atoning sacrifice for His people.
**Key site pages:**
- /systematic-atonement.html — Definite atonement explained
- /apologetic-definite-atonement.html — For whom did Christ die?
- /question-isaiah53.html — Isaiah 53 deep exegesis
- /devotional-my-chains-fell-away.html — Chains broken at the cross

**Newsletter idea (format B — Scripture Spotlight):** Isaiah 53:5 — "He was crushed for our iniquities." Focus on the word "our" — definite, particular, personal. He wasn't crushed for a theoretical humanity. He was crushed for you, by name.

**Social hooks:**
- "Good Friday isn't the day God made salvation *possible.* It's the day He *accomplished* it."
- "He didn't die to make you saveable. He died to save you." → link to /systematic-atonement.html

### Easter Sunday — April 20
**Theme:** Resurrection as proof of effectual redemption. He rose *because the work was finished.*
**Key site pages:**
- /systematic-resurrection.html
- /story-the-dead-city.html — The dead made alive (perfect Easter allegory)
- /analogy-lazarus-grave.html — Lazarus as picture of regeneration
- /devotional-dead-who-live.html

**Newsletter idea (format C — The Story):** "The Dead City" — retold as an Easter meditation. The Voice that calls dead people to life is the same Voice that called Lazarus out of the tomb and the same Voice that spoke on Easter morning.

**Social hooks:**
- "Easter isn't just about Jesus rising. It's about you rising. Because the same power that raised Christ is the power that made you alive." (Eph 2:4-6)
- "Lazarus didn't choose to walk out of the tomb. Jesus called, and the dead obeyed. That's the gospel." → /analogy-lazarus-grave.html

**Key site pages:**
- /systematic-pneumatology.html — Doctrine of the Holy Spirit

## OCTOBER 2026

### Reformation Day — October 31
**Theme:** The recovery of the gospel of grace. Sola Gratia, Sola Fide.
**Key site pages:**
- /history-luther.html — Luther's breakthrough
- /history-calvin-geneva.html — Calvin and Geneva
- /history-dort.html — The Synod of Dort
- /history-confessions-story.html — The story behind the confessions
- /theologian-luther.html, /theologian-calvin.html

**Newsletter idea (format D — Did You Know?):** On October 31, 1517, a monk nailed a document to a door. But the real revolution wasn't about indulgences — it was about one question: Can a dead soul save itself?

**Social hooks:**
- "Reformation Day > Halloween. 507 years ago, a monk rediscovered that salvation is all of grace."
- "Luther didn't invent sovereign grace. He rediscovered it — buried under centuries of works-righteousness."
