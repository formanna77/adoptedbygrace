Identity & Worth

The Broken Mirror

The world offers two answers to "Who am I?" — "You are whatever you decide to be" or "You are your achievements." Both are lies. Scripture offers a third answer so much better it will stop you mid-scroll: You are who God made you to be before you existed.

You Are Not Self-Made

Romans 9:23 is the foundation of this series: "What if God, desiring to show his wrath and to make known his power, has endured with much patience vessels of wrath prepared for destruction, in order to make known the riches of his glory for vessels of mercy, which he has prepared beforehand for glory?"

But the heart of it is simpler and more stunning: God prepared you beforehand. Not after you existed. Not based on what you would choose. Before. The foundation of the world was not laid yesterday. Neither was your identity.

In a world that screams "create yourself," Scripture whispers something radically different: "Discover who I made you to be." That's not a limitation — it's the most liberating sentence you'll ever hear. When your worth isn't earned, your purpose isn't invented, and your story isn't random, you're finally free from the exhausting project of self-construction.

"I praise you because I am fearfully and wonderfully made; your works are wonderful, I know that full well. My frame was not hidden from you when I was made in the secret place, when I was woven together in the depths of the earth. Your eyes saw my unformed body; all the days ordained for me were written in your book before one of them came to be."

Psalm 139:14–16 (NIV)
