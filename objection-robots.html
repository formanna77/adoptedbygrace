Questions

Does Predestination Make Us Robots?

The most common objection to sovereignty confuses two things that are nothing alike: a machine executing code and a person acting from transformed desires.

The Answer: A robot acts without desire, consciousness, or love. A regenerated believer acts from a new heart that genuinely wants God. Sovereignty does not override your will — it creates your will. Before grace, you were dead in sin, enslaved to desires that could only destroy you. Irresistible grace did not take your freedom away. It gave you freedom for the first time. And the Greek word for "workmanship" in Ephesians 2:10 is poiēma — poem. You are not God's machine. You are God's poem.

The Question That Dismantles Itself

The moment you explain that God chose His people before the foundation of the world, that salvation is entirely His initiative, that even faith is His gift — someone will interrupt: "So we're just robots, then?"

The objection feels powerful because the word "robot" does all the work. It smuggles in images of lifeless metal, cold determinism, and the obliteration of everything human. But here is the question the objection never asks — the one that dismantles it: when Shakespeare writes a character who falls in love, is that character a robot? When a novelist creates a figure so real that readers cry at their death, is the character less real because every word was chosen by someone else? Sovereignty is not coercion. It is authorship. And the most beautiful stories ever told are the ones where every detail was intended.

There is a deeper problem still. The objection assumes you were free before God acted. Scripture says you were in chains. The unregenerate will is not neutral — it is enslaved to sin (John 8:34). It can no more choose God than a dead man can choose to breathe. Irresistible grace did not take your freedom away. It gave you freedom for the first time.

What a Robot Actually Is — and What You Are Not

A robot acts without desire, consciousness, relationship, or joy. It does not love its task. It does not grieve when it fails. It does not worship its maker. Now open your Bible:

"I delight to do your will, O my God; your law is within my heart."

PSALM 40:8

"We love because he first loved us."

1 JOHN 4:19

The biblical picture is a person delighting, desiring, loving. These are the most deeply human activities imaginable. The fact that God initiated them — that He "first loved us" — does not make them robotic. It makes them miraculous. God does not drag His people kicking and screaming into the kingdom. He gives them new hearts that want to come. A person with a new heart who chooses God is the freest person in the room — because their desires have finally been aligned with reality.

Jesus Was Predestined — and He Was Not a Robot

The cross was the most predestined event in history. Peter says it plainly: "This man was handed over to you by God's deliberate plan and foreknowledge" (Acts 2:23). Everything that happened — Herod, Pilate, the soldiers, the mob — happened because God's hand and plan "had decided beforehand should happen" (Acts 4:27-28).

Was Jesus a robot in Gethsemane? Was His agony mechanical? Was His cry — "Not my will, but yours be done" — the output of a program? Every Christian instinctively knows the answer is no. Jesus was the most fully human person who ever lived, and every moment of His life was predestined by the Father. If predestination did not make the Son of God a robot, it does not make you one either.

The Real Robots

Here is the irony the objection misses entirely. Scripture describes the unregenerate person — the one supposedly exercising "free" will — in terms that sound far more robotic than anything said about the elect:

"As for you, you were dead in your transgressions and sins, in which you used to live when you followed the ways of this world and of the ruler of the kingdom of the air."

EPHESIANS 2:1-2

"Dead." "Following the ways." "Following the ruler." This is a person on autopilot — driven by sin, enslaved to the world-system, unable to choose otherwise. Slaves are closer to robots than any saved person will ever be. The doctrines of grace say God frees His people from that autopilot. He breaks the program. He reboots the heart. He gives genuine desires, real affections, actual love. Election is the end of robotic existence, not the beginning of it.

You Are a Poem

Paul writes in Ephesians 2:10: "For we are God's handiwork, created in Christ Jesus to do good works, which God prepared in advance for us to do." The word translated "handiwork" is the Greek poiēma — from which English derives "poem." You are not God's machine. You are God's poem.

A poem is not a robot. A poem is an artwork of intentional beauty where every word was chosen by the author — and the result is not cold mechanics but living meaning. A painting is not less beautiful because the painter chose every brushstroke. It is beautiful because the painter chose every brushstroke. When God predestined you, He was not writing code. He was writing poetry.

The Strongest Objection — Answered

"Even if God gives new desires, the outcome was still determined before we were born. The person could not have done otherwise. And if you cannot do otherwise, you are not genuinely free."

This is the libertarian free will position, and it deserves a serious response. Consider: a woman who marries the man she loves was never going to choose differently — she loved him. But no one would call her a robot because she "couldn't have done otherwise." She did exactly what her deepest desires led her to do. Her choice was determined by who she is, and that made it more free, not less.

Philosophers call this compatibilism: genuine freedom is acting according to your desires without external coercion — even if those desires were given to you by God. It is the position held by Augustine, Luther, Calvin, Edwards, Spurgeon, and the vast majority of the Christian tradition. God's sovereignty and human responsibility are not enemies. They are partners. You are free because God made you free.

What This Means for You

If you are a believer, your faith is not the output of a program. It is the gift of a Father who loved you before time began. Your choices are real. Your love for God is genuine. Your tears in worship, your desire to obey, your grief over sin — these are not simulations. They are the fruit of a new heart that God Himself planted in your chest.

You are not a robot. You are poiēma — a masterwork. A poem God has been writing since before the stars existed. He did not decree you into existence like a programmer typing code. He chose you like a Father claims a child. He loved you like a Groom pursues a Bride. He ransomed you like a King pays a price for treasure.

The robot objection fears that predestination is cold and mechanical. But Scripture shows something far more intimate. The God who predestined every detail of your salvation is the same God who sings over you with joy (Zephaniah 3:17), calls you by name (Isaiah 43:1), and wipes tears from your eyes (Revelation 21:4). That is not the story of a machine. That is the story of grace. And the One who wrote it will never give up on you.

"For we are God's handiwork, created in Christ Jesus to do good works, which God prepared in advance for us to do."

EPHESIANS 2:10
