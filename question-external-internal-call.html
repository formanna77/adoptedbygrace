The Call You Can Ignore vs. The Call That Creates Life

If God is sovereign, why does the Bible command everyone to repent? Because there are two calls — and confusing them is the most common mistake in the entire debate.

In Brief: The external call is the gospel preached to all — genuine, universal, and resistible. The internal call is the Spirit's work in the heart — specific, effectual, and irresistible. Both are in your Bible. Both are true. The external call explains human responsibility. The internal call explains why anyone believes at all.

A riverbank in Philippi. Sabbath morning. A group of women have gathered to pray by the water, their voices low, the current sliding past. A man named Paul begins to speak — the same gospel he has preached in a dozen cities. The same words. The same truth. And then something happens that no one in the group can see: "The Lord opened her heart to respond to Paul's message" (Acts 16:14). One woman — Lydia, a dealer in purple cloth — hears the same syllables the others hear. But she hears them differently. Something behind the words reaches into her chest and turns a key she did not know was locked.

That moment — that invisible, sovereign, irreversible opening — is the hinge of the entire gospel. And it raises a question most Christians have never thought to ask.

Jesus stands on the Mount of Olives and weeps over Jerusalem: "How often I have longed to gather your children together, as a hen gathers her chicks under her wings, and you were not willing" (Matthew 23:37). His desire was genuine. His tears were real.

But if God is sovereign — if He controls all things — how could Jerusalem be "not willing"? Didn't He foreknow they would reject Him? Didn't He ordain the events of history?

The resolution is not complicated. It is beautiful. There are two calls — and they are not in conflict. They are two movements of the same symphony.

The External Call

The external call is the proclamation of the gospel to all people. It goes to everyone — the elect and the non-elect, the willing and the unwilling. It is genuine. Jesus really offered Himself to Jerusalem. His tears were not performance.

The external call is public, makes real promises, reflects God's character, and can be refused. Those who reject it are held accountable because it was genuine.

The Internal Call

The internal call is something entirely different. It is the inward work of the Holy Spirit by which God resurrects the very soul He commands to live.

"The Lord opened her heart to respond to Paul's message."

ACTS 16:14

Paul preached the external call to Lydia. But then something more happened. "The Lord opened her heart." The Spirit's invisible, sovereign work within her spirit. She believed. She was baptized. Her household followed.

"No one can come to me unless the Father who sent me draws them, and I will raise them up at the last day."

JOHN 6:44

The word "draws" is not a passive suggestion — it is active, powerful, effectual. Those whom the Father draws come. No uncertainty. The Father's internal call produces faith and secures salvation. It is irresistible — not through coercion, but through transformation of desires. Those who receive it cannot help but respond, because they have been given new hearts that want to respond.

How Both Work Together

Two people hear the same sermon. One weeps. One shrugs. If the difference is the hearer, then the hearer is the hero. Is that the gospel?

The external call explains why we preach to everyone. The internal call explains why anyone actually believes. The external call demonstrates human responsibility. The internal call demonstrates God's sovereignty.

The external call goes to all. Where the internal call is absent, people hear the gospel but do not respond. Where the internal call is present, the Spirit opens the heart, and faith follows.

Jesus's tears express the real emotional reality of God the Son encountering human resistance to His genuine offer. His sovereignty is not thwarted — because the cross itself was the centerpiece of God's redemptive plan. The external call and the internal call are not enemies. They are partners in salvation.

Why This Changes Everything

When you understand the two calls, the contradictions dissolve. You can preach with genuine urgency, knowing your proclamation serves God's plan. You can call people to faith with real passion, knowing their response is their responsibility. And you can rest in God's sovereignty.

The external call is the invitation. The internal call is the resurrection.

One you can refuse. The other creates the very life it commands.

Go back to the riverbank. The water is still moving. Paul is still speaking. The other women hear his words and nod, or look away, or think about the market later that afternoon. But Lydia's eyes have changed. Something behind the syllables reached her — not louder, not more eloquent, but alive in a way the external sound waves alone could never be. She believed. Not because she was smarter, not because she was more spiritual, not because she tried harder. Because the Lord opened her heart.

He called you from the inside out.

And that call has never been recalled.
