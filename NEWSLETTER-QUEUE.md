## Newsletter: The Orphanage — A Story About the Day You Didn't Choose
**Date drafted:** 2026-03-30
**Source page(s):** /story-the-orphanage.html, /question-faithgift.html
**Format:** C. "The Story"
**Status:** READY FOR REVIEW

---

There was an orphanage where every child was told the same beautiful lie.

*When Adoption Day comes, a line of families will stand before you — and you will choose.*

The children rehearsed it like gospel. They brushed their hair, practiced smiling, competed to be charming. They believed the most important moment of their lives would hinge on their performance. That when the door opened and the families appeared, the child who had prepared hardest, wanted it most, reached out first — that child would be chosen.

It gave them dignity. It gave them hope. And it was completely wrong.

Because when Adoption Day actually came for a girl named Lila, something happened that no one had prepared her for. The families didn't line up for her inspection. A single father walked in, called her by name — a name she hadn't told anyone — and said, "I've come for you."

She hadn't chosen him. She didn't even know he existed. He had chosen her. And he had chosen her before she was born.

---

I think about this story often because it mirrors something Paul wrote to the Philippians from a Roman prison cell. He told them that their faith — the very believing that saved them — had been *granted* to them. The Greek word is *echaristhe*: graciously given. Not earned. Not decided into existence. Given.

"For it has been granted to you that for the sake of Christ you should not only believe in him but also suffer for his sake." — Philippians 1:29

We want to be the child who chose wisely. But Scripture keeps insisting we are the child who was called by name.

This is either the most offensive idea in the world or the most liberating. Offensive, because it strips us of credit. Liberating, because it means our salvation doesn't depend on the strength of our choosing — it depends on the faithfulness of the One who chose us.

Ephesians 2:8–9 seals it: "For by grace you have been saved through faith. And this is not your own doing; it is the gift of God, not a result of works, so that no one may boast."

The faith that saves you is not your contribution to the transaction. It is God's gift to you. You are not the customer — you are the rescued. You didn't walk into the adoption agency and pick a Father. The Father walked into your orphanage and called your name.

And here's what changes everything: if He chose you, He will keep you. A father who crossed heaven and earth to adopt you is not going to lose the paperwork.
