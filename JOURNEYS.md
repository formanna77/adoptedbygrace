# JOURNEYS — The Paths Through the Site

> Eight minds and hearts the site is built to welcome. Each section names a kind of reader, the doorway that suits them, the path of pages that unfolds the doctrines of grace in the order their posture can bear, and the gaps where the path isn't yet whole.
>
> **The standard.** Every page along every path is meant to be a gem in its own right — dense with wisdom, airtight in logic, beautiful in prose, Scripture-load-bearing, worth reading whether the reader is eighteen or eighty, whether it's morning or midnight. A journey is not a funnel. It is a route through a cathedral, and every stone of the cathedral should reward the one who stops to look at it.
>
> **Use this doc to** pick one reader, walk their path as they would, and patch every place the path breaks — usually by adding two or three prose links from existing pages to existing pages. Do not build new pages from this doc; new pages belong to `SEARCH-INTENT.md`.
>
> All page references were verified on disk as of 2026-04-21.

## Reader 1 — The Seeker Without a Framework

> *Comes to the site with no theological vocabulary. Has not studied Calvinism, Arminianism, or any "-ism." Carries a longing for God that is real but unnamed. Will not recognize TULIP if you drop it on them, and does not need to. What they need is the same thing everyone needs: to meet a God who was kinder and more sovereign than they knew.*

**Entry point:** [/found-you.html](/found-you.html) — the unadorned doorway for a reader who does not yet speak the language.

**Path:**
1. [/found-you.html](/found-you.html) — opens with the discovery that rescue came to them before they knew to ask for it.
2. → [/devotional-the-hands-that-hold-you.html](/devotional-the-hands-that-hold-you.html) — the assurance that they were held even when they did not know they were held.
3. → [/devotional-chosen-before-you-were-broken.html](/devotional-chosen-before-you-were-broken.html) — the brokenness reframed as the thing the choosing preceded, not the thing that disqualified them from being chosen.
4. → [/devotional-never-gives-up.html](/devotional-never-gives-up.html) — the most-backlinked page on the site, and for good reason: perseverance read as promise, not command.
5. → [/story-tender-the-letter-already-written.html](/story-tender-the-letter-already-written.html) — narrative form, which this reader receives more easily than propositional form.
6. → [/two-arms.html](/two-arms.html) — the architecture, named at the hour they can hear it.
7. → [/start-here.html](/start-here.html) — the broader map, for the reader ready to walk further.

**Gaps:**

- Audit `found-you.html` to ensure it closes with at least three outbound links into the path above; the page is the only shared entrance for this reader, and a closing without paths is a door without a hallway.

## Reader 2 — The Shaken Arminian

> *Has lived inside the Arminian framework for years, often for decades. Something recently has cracked it — a sermon, a verse read in a different light, a slow weight they could no longer argue out of themselves. Knows the vocabulary well and is allergic to triumphalism. The right move is not to win an argument. The right move is to let Scripture and logic do what they are already doing.*

**Entry point:** [/for-arminians.html](/for-arminians.html) for the reader who wants a pastoral doorway, or [/question-faithgift.html](/question-faithgift.html) for the reader whose crack began at Ephesians 2:8–9.

**Path:**
1. [/for-arminians.html](/for-arminians.html) or [/question-faithgift.html](/question-faithgift.html) — meet them at the precise place the crack began.
2. → [/question-where-did-your-faith-come-from.html](/question-where-did-your-faith-come-from.html) — the Socratic question that cannot be answered from inside the Arminian framework without conceding the game.
3. → [/argument-arminianism-secretly-assumes-calvinism.html](/argument-arminianism-secretly-assumes-calvinism.html) — the long-form logical move (5,645 words) that walks them step by step to the conclusion they already half-suspect.
4. → [/compare-calvinism-arminianism.html](/compare-calvinism-arminianism.html) — a comparison conducted without polemic; the reader makes the judgment.
5. → [/argument-arminian-sermon-illustrations-refuted.html](/argument-arminian-sermon-illustrations-refuted.html) — the illustrations they grew up with, examined in daylight.
6. → [/letters-senior-demon-arminianism.html](/letters-senior-demon-arminianism.html) — Lewis-style satire; sometimes a sideways light does what direct argument cannot.
7. → [/objection-fairness.html](/objection-fairness.html) and [/objection-responsibility.html](/objection-responsibility.html) — the last two defenses, answered in turn.
8. → [/devotional-rescued-without-a-say.html](/devotional-rescued-without-a-say.html) — the tender landing for the reader whose framework has come apart. The arms that caught them were there the whole time.

**Gaps:**
- Verify `for-arminians.html` (currently 600w, 9 inbound) links explicitly to every page on the path above. It is mid-tier in weight; it should be raised to a Hammer page for this reader specifically.
- Ensure step 7 links forward to the catch in step 8; a reader who has just watched their framework dissolve should not be left in the rubble without a hand.

## Reader 3 — The One Who Was Promised the Wrong Gospel

> *Taught that God would heal every sickness, grant every prayer, and reward every ounce of faith with visible blessing. Life did not deliver what the framework promised. The result is not usually a tidy deconstruction but a deeper grief — the sense that either God failed or the framework lied, and either answer is unbearable. The true doctrines of grace, delivered with tenderness, turn out to be the only framework sturdy enough to hold what actually happened.*

**Entry point:** [/for-hurting.html](/for-hurting.html) or [/open-wound-hub.html](/open-wound-hub.html) — both open with the wound before the theology.

**Path:**
1. [/for-hurting.html](/for-hurting.html) or [/open-wound-hub.html](/open-wound-hub.html) — the wound is named first and without hurry.
2. → [/open-wound-the-prayer-god-didnt-answer.html](/open-wound-the-prayer-god-didnt-answer.html) — the specific grief this reader came with.
3. → [/open-wound-sovereignty-in-grief.html](/open-wound-sovereignty-in-grief.html) — sovereignty read not as cruelty but as the only floor strong enough to stand on when life has shattered. *(Currently thin — flagged below.)*
4. → [/psychology-cost-of-rejecting-grace.html](/psychology-cost-of-rejecting-grace.html) — what the prosperity framework actually costs, measured in souls.
5. → [/devotional-rescued-without-a-say.html](/devotional-rescued-without-a-say.html) — the catch.
6. → [/devotional-the-hands-that-hold-you.html](/devotional-the-hands-that-hold-you.html) — assurance that does not depend on outcomes or on the reader's performance of joy.
7. → [/systematic-divine-decrees.html](/systematic-divine-decrees.html) — when they are ready for the architecture, the doctrine of decrees gives them a God who is not caught off guard by any of it.
8. → [/theologian-spurgeon.html](/theologian-spurgeon.html) — Spurgeon's depressions stand as proof that sovereign grace does not require cheerfulness.

**Gaps:**
- `open-wound-sovereignty-in-grief.html` is 227 words. This is the load-bearing page of the whole path, and the one where sovereignty and suffering have to be made to sing the same song. Expand to 1,500 words or more, in Spurgeon's key, not in a seminary's. Highest-priority single expansion on the site.
- `for-hurting.html` (786w, 5 inbound) should be raised to Hammer weight for this reader.
- Add, within `for-hurting.html` or as a sibling, an explicit welcome to the reader who was specifically taught the prosperity gospel. Naming that framework directly is part of dismantling it.

## Reader 4 — The Cold Cradle Calvinist

> *Grew up Reformed. Could recite the Five Points before they could drive. Knows the catechism cold. And the affections never ignited. This reader does not need another doctrine; they need the doctrines they already confess to burn in them the way they burned in Edwards, in Bunyan, in the Puritans who wept over the same pages this reader reads without pulse.*

**Entry point:** [/joy-hub.html](/joy-hub.html) — under-leveraged at present and badly in need of expansion.

**Path:**
1. [/joy-hub.html](/joy-hub.html) — the hub meant to wake the affections of the doctrinally right but devotionally cold.
2. → [/devotional-joy-of-election.html](/devotional-joy-of-election.html) (3,014 words) — joy rooted specifically in unconditional election, not in vague gospel sentiment.
3. → [/devotional-forever-loved.html](/devotional-forever-loved.html) (2,620 words) — the love that stood behind every doctrine this reader has ever correctly recited.

5. → [/theologian-edwards.html](/theologian-edwards.html) — Edwards on the religious affections, the reader's own tradition reaching through time to shake them awake.
6. → [/theologian-spurgeon.html](/theologian-spurgeon.html) — Spurgeon's tenderness as the element a dry Reformed orthodoxy most often lacks.
7. → [/devotional-love-letter-before-time.html](/devotional-love-letter-before-time.html) — close with the voice of the Bridegroom, not the voice of the lecture hall.
8. → [/the-breath-prayer.html](/the-breath-prayer.html) — a prayer for the reader whose heart has not been used in a while. *(Currently thin — flagged below.)*

**Gaps:**
- `joy-hub.html` is only 674 words with 21 inbound; the inbound count is strong, the outbound interior is thin. Expand it into a proper doorway, with an opener for the reader who confesses the doctrines and cannot remember the last time one of them moved them.
