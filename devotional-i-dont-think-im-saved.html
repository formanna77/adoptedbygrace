I Don't Think I'm Saved

You're awake at 2am, terrified. This page is for you.

In Brief

The fact that you are terrified about your salvation is among the strongest possible evidence that you are saved. The dead do not mourn the absence of life. A corpse does not worry about its pulse. Your anguish is not a sign of spiritual death — it is a sign of spiritual life. And your salvation was never built on the strength of your grip. It was built on God's choice, sealed by Christ's blood, and sustained by the Spirit's power.

The Fear Itself Is the Evidence

You can't sleep. The thought sits on your chest like a stone: I don't think I'm really saved. Maybe it's been a low hum your entire Christian life, and tonight it broke through the surface.

Here is the first thing you need to hear: the fact that you are terrified about your salvation is among the strongest possible evidence that you are saved.

Think about it. The person who is truly far from God — no conviction from the Spirit, no hunger for assurance — is not lying awake in agony about their eternal state. They are sleeping soundly. They are not Googling "am I really saved." A corpse does not worry about its pulse. But you are aching. The dead do not search for the living. You are searching. Something in you is alive enough to be terrified of losing what it has.

"For it is God who works in you to will and to act in order to fulfill his good purpose."

PHILIPPIANS 2:13

That desperate need to know that God is real and that you belong to Him? You did not manufacture that. Where did that longing come from? The caring was done to you. The very desire for assurance is a gift from the God you're afraid has abandoned you.

Your Doubt Does Not Disqualify You

When doubt hits, your mind tells a story: Real Christians don't doubt. Real Christians feel God's presence. I don't feel it. Therefore I'm not real. Every sentence in that story is a lie.

David — the man after God's own heart — wrote Psalm 13: "How long, Lord? Will you forget me forever?" That is the prayer of a man lying awake at 2am. The greatest saints in history went through seasons of devastating spiritual darkness. Mother Teresa's private letters revealed fifty years of feeling God's absence. Your doubt puts you in the company of the most honest believers who ever lived.

Your anguish about salvation is evidence for it, not against it. If you had no spiritual life, no indwelling Spirit, no new nature — you would have no capacity to grieve your spiritual state. A person with no taste for God does not ache when they can't find Him. The ache proves the taste exists. The grief proves the relationship is real.

"Being confident of this, that he who began a good work in you will carry it on to completion until the day of Christ Jesus."

PHILIPPIANS 1:6

It does not say "until the day you fail badly enough." It says completion. The work is God's. The carrying is God's. You are not the sustainer of your salvation. Your doubt does not interrupt His work. Your doubt is part of the terrain He is carrying you through.

But What About My Sin?

Maybe the doubt isn't abstract. Maybe you're staring at a specific sin — something so dark you can barely name it. And the voice says: A real Christian wouldn't do this. You've gone too far.

God chose you before the foundation of the world. Before Genesis 1:1. Before there was a planet for you to sin on. He saw every failure, every rebellion, every shameful secret — and He chose you anyway. Not despite knowing. While knowing. His choice was made with full disclosure. No surprises. No take-backs.

"My sheep listen to my voice; I know them, and they follow me. I give them eternal life, and they shall never perish; no one will snatch them out of my hand."

JOHN 10:27-28

Read those words again. "They shall never perish." Not "rarely perish." Not "unless they sin badly enough." It is not your grip on God that matters. It is His grip on you. You are in His hand. And His Father's hand is around His. No one can snatch you out.

"But what if I'm not one of His sheep?" you ask. The goats never ask it.

When You Can't Feel It

Perhaps the most agonizing part is the silence. You pray and feel nothing. You read Scripture and the words lie flat. You watch others worship with tears and you feel nothing. The absence of feeling becomes, in your mind, the absence of God.

But your salvation was never built on your feelings. Feelings come and go. The felt presence of God ebbs and flows. There are seasons of warmth and seasons of winter. The winter does not mean the sun has died. It means the earth has turned.

The founder of this site lost the felt presence of God for over a decade. He ran. He rebelled. He traveled a dozen countries trying to find a softer reality. His quiet prayer through all of it: "Please, don't let me go." And God didn't. Not through rebellion. Not through exile. Not through years of silence. The grip held. It always holds.

The Question Underneath the Question

The question on the surface is: Am I saved? The question underneath is: Is my salvation something I am holding onto, or something that is holding onto me?

Notice what your fear assumes. It assumes that somewhere in the equation there is a thread you are responsible for keeping tight — a thread of belief, or sincerity, or adequate faith. The reason you are terrified is that you have looked down at the thread and it looks frayed. You have measured your own faith and found it thin. So the math comes out to lost.

But your fear has smuggled in an entire theology you never agreed to. It has smuggled in the assumption that there is a part of your salvation you are holding up. That if you let go of your end, the bridge collapses. That God did His part at the cross and now you are doing yours by believing hard enough.

If even one molecule of your eternal security depended on the strength of your grip, you would already be lost. Not in twenty years. Tonight. The grip of a person who lies awake terrified that their grip is slipping is — by definition — not the kind of grip that can hold up an eternity. You are not lying awake afraid that God failed. You are lying awake afraid that you did. Which means somewhere you have been told that you are responsible for the holding.

You are not responsible for the holding. You never were.

The bridge over the abyss is not a contract. It is a Person. And the Person is not gripping your hand from the other side waiting to see if you can keep up. He is carrying you across in His arms, and the entire weight of your salvation is on Him. The only reason you are even capable of being terrified about losing Him is that He has already put His Spirit inside you — and that Spirit is, even now, refusing to let you settle for a god who could be lost.

Stop asking whether your faith is strong enough to hold onto God. Start asking whether God is strong enough to hold onto you. The moment you ask the second question, you already know the answer — because the only reason you would ever ask it is that He already is.

"For I am convinced that neither death nor life, neither angels nor demons, neither the present nor the future, nor any powers, neither height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God that is in Christ Jesus our Lord."

ROMANS 8:38-39

"Nothing in all creation" includes you. You cannot un-choose yourself. Only God can un-choose you. And He won't.
