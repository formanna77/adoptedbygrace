History

The Reformed Resurgence

How sovereign grace survived modernity: Machen, Lloyd-Jones, Sproul, MacArthur — and a generation that found the old faith alive.

In Brief

By 1900, liberal theology had gutted the mainline denominations from the inside. The virgin birth was "mythology." The cross was "a moral example." God was a feeling, not a Person. Into this wreckage, God raised up defenders — Machen, Lloyd-Jones, Sproul, MacArthur — who refused to let the doctrines of grace die. By the twenty-first century, a new generation discovered the old faith was alive. The resurgence is not over. It is accelerating.

The Liberal Crisis

By 1900, the church had been quietly bleeding for half a century and most of its members had no idea. The arteries were cut not by atheists but by pastors — men in clerical collars who still prayed on Sundays and no longer believed a word of what they were praying. The virgin birth was mythology. The resurrection was a spiritual metaphor. The cross was a moving example of self-giving love. And the people in the pews kept tithing, kept singing, kept assuming the gospel they learned as children was still being preached.

Meanwhile, the men in the pulpits had already stopped believing it.

By the 1920s, the great Protestant denominations had been largely captured. The seminaries, the publishing houses, the denominational structures — all had fallen into the hands of men who no longer believed the faith of the apostles. The truth that God alone saves, through sovereign grace, by the work of Christ, applied by irresistible grace — was being systematically abandoned. In its place came a humanistic religion that made man the measure and rendered God a vague force of moral improvement.

Machen: Defender of the Faith

J. Gresham Machen (1881-1937) taught at Princeton Theological Seminary when liberal theology began dominating even Princeton's halls. His 1923 book Christianity and Liberalism made an argument that remains radical: liberalism is not a form of Christianity at all. It is a different religion entirely. Liberalism is built on human reason; Christianity on revelation. Liberalism denies the supernatural; Christianity is saturated with it.

When Princeton fell under liberal control, Machen left and helped found Westminster Theological Seminary in 1929 — an institution dedicated to the Reformed faith. He also founded the Orthodox Presbyterian Church when the mainline would not discipline heretical ministers. Machen's genius was saying what everyone could see but no one dared name: the emperor had a PhD and no clothes. He proved that orthodox Christianity could defend itself not through accommodation but through faithful exposition of Scripture. Machen showed that the doctrines of grace were not relics but living truth for every age.

Lloyd-Jones: Preacher to the Preachers

Martyn Lloyd-Jones (1899-1981) was a physician turned preacher who spent thirty years at Westminster Chapel in London. Ministers from across the Protestant world came to learn from him. He devoted thirteen years to expounding Romans — line by line, showing how Paul articulates the gospel of sovereign grace with precision and power.

Lloyd-Jones was convinced the church needed revival — not reformed structures but a mighty movement of the Spirit. And he believed revival comes through faithful proclamation of biblical truth. When believers understand they have been chosen before the creation of the world, that Christ died specifically for them, that the Spirit irresistibly changed their hearts — the result is not arrogance but humility, not complacency but zeal. In an ecumenical age of accommodation, Lloyd-Jones stood firm: you could be a great preacher not by watering down the message but by understanding Scripture deeply and proclaiming it with power.

Sproul and MacArthur: Teaching a Generation

R.C. Sproul (1939-2017) founded Ligonier Ministries with one conviction: the church desperately needs to understand the doctrines of grace, and these truths can be taught with clarity even in a television age. His genius was making deep theology understandable — explaining unconditional election so a child could grasp it and a scholar could respect it. Millions heard him. Thousands discovered they were Calvinists without knowing it — that their faith rested on God's sovereignty, not on human choice.

John MacArthur has preached at Grace Community Church for over fifty years, expounding Scripture verse by verse. He has never shied from unpopular truths — preaching election when the culture demanded autonomy, defending the sufficiency of Scripture when the world insisted human wisdom must supplement revelation, standing against the seeker-sensitive movement and the prosperity gospel. His conviction is simple: the doctrines of grace are not negotiable. They are the teaching of Scripture, and Scripture is the Word of God.

Young, Restless, and Reformed

By the early twenty-first century, something unexpected happened. A generation born after 1980 began discovering the doctrines of grace. They read Edwards. They studied the Westminster Confession. They listened to Piper and Keller. They realized the faith of the Reformation, the faith of the Puritans, the faith defended by Machen and Lloyd-Jones and Sproul was not antiquated. But here is the question that haunts church history: Why does every generation that rediscovers sovereign grace feel like it is discovering something new? Because the previous generation buried it.

The movement proved something crucial: the doctrines of grace have not lost their power. When presented faithfully, grounded in Scripture, shown to produce humility and gratitude and holiness — they resonate. People want to know their salvation rests on something more solid than their own willpower. They want to belong to a faith that makes absolute claims. Reformed churches are growing in Africa, Asia, and Latin America. Young pastors worldwide are studying Reformed theology. The gospel of sovereign grace is spreading more widely than ever before — because the God who preserved it through every century is the God who holds His people with hands that will never let go.

"Timothy, guard what has been entrusted to your care. Turn away from godless chatter and the opposing ideas of what is falsely called knowledge."

1 TIMOTHY 6:20

Guard the deposit. The truth has survived every century that tried to kill it. It will survive this one.
