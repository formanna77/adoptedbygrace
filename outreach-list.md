# OUTREACH-LIST — Phase 5 Workstream 4 (External Lighthouse Building)

> Initial pass written 2026-04-27 (Session 34). The list is a living document; every column expands as Aaron makes contact.
>
> **Scope.** Reformed publications, pastors, podcasts, ministries, and academic journals whose audiences overlap with adoptedbygrace.net's mission. The site is doctrinally Reformed, soteriologically the doctrines of grace, and stylistically Lewis/Spurgeon/Buechner/Keller/Edwards/Chesterton. Pitches must be tuned to that posture — not "we have a Calvinist blog" but "we are building the most powerful instrument of biblical truth on the internet on the doctrines of grace, and we want to honor your audience by sending them something worth their time."
>
> **What this list does NOT do.** It does not handle outreach for you. It does not draft cover letters. It identifies the lighthouse, the contact path, and the one-sentence pitch. From there, Aaron writes the email, makes the call, sends the link.
>
> **What "lighthouse" means here.** A lighthouse is a publication, pastor, podcast, or ministry already trusted by readers in our orbit. We do not need them to amplify us; we need them to know we exist as a referral resource. *"You have an article on the doctrines of grace already? Here is one more, beautifully written, that complements yours. Free to share, free to link."* That is the pitch shape.

## Outreach principles (read before sending anything)

1. **Lead with a specific page, not "the site."** Pitch: "I think your Romans 9 readers would benefit from this single printable — *here is the URL*." Not: "Please link to my site." A specific, beautiful artifact is what gets clicked. The site at large is what they discover after the click.

2. **Send the printable as a PDF attachment, not a link only.** Editors and pastors are busy. A single-page PDF that prints well is harder to ignore than a URL.

3. **Quote one of their pieces in your pitch.** "Your essay last March on irresistible grace was the cleanest I've read in five years; the printable I attached was written by someone in the same lineage." Specificity proves you read them.

4. **Never ask for amplification. Always offer a resource.** The pitch is "here is something free your readers might use," not "please tell your readers about us." The first lands; the second goes to spam.

5. **Track every send.** A simple spreadsheet — date sent, contact, channel, response — keeps the campaign honest and surfaces who is open to a follow-up six months out.

6. **Honor "no" with grace.** Most "nos" are silent. Some will be polite passes. A few will be opinionated. Every reply, even a no, is a relationship — file the contact for future occasions.

7. **Patience.** Lighthouses don't shine quickly. The first reply may take ninety days. The first link may take six months. The first podcast appearance may take a year. The mission is rescue, and rescue lighthouses are built one stone, one season, one careful conversation at a time.

## Open question for Aaron

The list above is curated; it is not exhaustive. Three categories Aaron should decide whether to pursue:

1. **Translation partnerships.** Several entries above mention translation. The site's prose translates well (it carves, it does not slog), but translation requires partnership. Worth a dedicated pass?

2. **Academic-grade lectures.** Some entries (Westminster, PRTS, TMS, Mid-America) suggest the site as a citation resource. A more ambitious pitch is a guest lecture / convocation talk on the Apex Rhetoric Protocol as a *writing* discipline rather than a theology discipline. Worth pursuing in a year or two when the site has more public reach?

3. **Print compendium.** A book version of the Hammer 50 — *Adopted by Grace: Fifty Hammer Spines on the Doctrines of Grace* — would be a Crossway / Banner-of-Truth / Reformation Heritage Books candidate. The work is already done; it would only need editorial curation. Worth deciding whether the site is the destination or whether the book is the eventual destination.

These questions do not block outreach. They shape what kind of outreach is most valuable.
