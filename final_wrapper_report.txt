ROOT CAUSES:

1. Non-standard wrapper class names:
   - answer-body (instead of article-body)
   - content (ambiguous, often standalone without max-width)
   - section / content-section (not the same as article-body wrapper)
   - definition, answer-header, arguments-grid (custom, no wrapper parent)

2. Sections appearing OUTSIDE any wrapper:
   - <section class="overview-table"> goes directly after <header>
   - <div class="section-nav"> appears between </nav> and hero
   - <section class="point-section"> not inside article-body
   - <div class="compare-grid"> sits without wrapper

3. Full-width layouts that break padding/centering:
   - Tables and grids render at 100% viewport width
   - No left/right padding on mobile
   - No max-width constraint means content extends edge-to-edge
   - Text becomes hard to read on mobile/tablets
