# SEARCH-INTENT — The Questions the Site Is Built to Answer
_Session 1 deliverable · adoptedbygrace.net · 2026-04-21 (revised for standard)_

## What this document is

A thoughtful reader will arrive at the doctrines of grace with specific questions — some intellectual, some aching, some phrased in the idiom of a theology class and some phrased in the idiom of an ordinary life. This document gathers those questions, maps each to the page on the site that answers it, and grades the answer. Where an answer is weak, a retune is recommended. Where no answer yet exists, a page is commissioned.

**The standard.** Every page named below — whether already written, waiting to be rewritten, or waiting to be built — is held to the same measure. Density of insight. Airtight logic. Scripture-load-bearing. Prose worthy of Lewis, Spurgeon, Buechner, Keller, Edwards, Chesterton. Every sentence earns its place. The reader rises to meet the page, not the page to the reader. If a new page ships that would not reward a careful, unhurried reading, it has not been built to the standard of this site and should be reworked.

**Discoverability is a consequence of quality, not a substitute for it.** A page that genuinely answers a question with depth will be found. The purpose of this map is to ensure the site's coverage is whole — that no natural question hits a broken answer or a missing one — and that each door carries the title that honestly names what is inside it.

**Grading.**
- **ANCHOR** — the page answers the question with depth appropriate to the subject, and its title and opening honestly name what the reader was asking.
- **ADEQUATE** — a page exists but its title, opening, or depth is below the standard; a retune will lift it to anchor.
- **PARTIAL** — a page covers the territory from one angle (logical, devotional, pastoral) but the question requires a companion page from the complementary angle.
- **GAP** — no page yet answers the question; a new page is required.

## Part IV — The Standard for Every New Page

Every page built from this map meets the site's standing contract in CLAUDE.md and the following five measures.

1. **Density.** Every paragraph says something no other paragraph says. No throat-clearing. No restating the question at the joints of the argument.
2. **Depth from multiple angles.** Scripture and at least two of the following: psychology, philosophy, history, neuroscience, analogy, testimony. The doctrines of grace are proved true from every angle; the site should prove it on every page.
3. **Beauty.** Sentences of varying length, cadence deliberate, nothing slack. Whatever the subject, the prose is worthy of the subject.
4. **Scripture carrying weight, not decoration.** NIV. No parenthetical "(NIV)." A quoted verse must be doing work the page's own words could not do.
5. **Two arms.** Demolition and catch in the same page, or a prominent link to the catch. No reader is left in rubble at the end of a page on this site.

Structural requirements (from CLAUDE.md) are separate and non-negotiable: canonical `<article class="article-body">` wrapper, nav template, `global.css`, `nav.js`, footer-grace-warning block, eight to twelve verified internal links woven into prose. No inline styles. No banned patterns (section-nav, article-toc, newsletter, social-share).
