### 2026-03-30 — ENHANCER (Session: Doctor-Corpse SEO Title Surgery) — CRAFT
**Discovery:** Analogy pages buried in literary titles ("Doctor and Corpse") perform zero SEO traffic despite hub getting 28 visits/week. Reframing to question-based titles ("Why a Dead Man Can't Choose God?") immediately targets search intent. The surgery involved three layers: (1) <title> tag shortened to 60 chars with question format, (2) og:title expanded to include explanation, (3) visible h1 changed to match search intent. All three work together. Pages that answer questions people actually Google always outrank pages that tell stories about analogies.
**Why it matters:** Every analogy page on the site probably has the same problem—clever literary title that teaches a lesson to someone ALREADY reading, but doesn't invite people searching for answers. Systematic audit of all analogy pages needed. Title-first design: ask "what question does this answer?" before choosing a headline.
**Apply to:** All remaining analogy pages (/analogy-liars, /analogy-marriage, /analogy-theater, etc.). Rewrite titles to question format. Keep the literary beauty IN the content, but lead with the QUESTION in the metadata and heading.

### 2026-03-30 — ENHANCER (Session: Doctor-Corpse SEO Title Surgery) — CRAFT
**Discovery:** Adding 3+ "Devastating Questions" (not rhetorical flourishes but actual reader-facing questions that force re-evaluation) increases engagement hooks. Deployed: (1) "If you are spiritually dead, can you choose God?" (before section on limitations), (2) "Did you believe BEFORE God raised you, or did the believing happen BECAUSE God raised you?" (after 1 John 5:1), (3) "Think about your own conversion..." (after Scripture anchor). Questions are NOT highlighted in callouts. They're embedded in prose naturally. This differs from the older "Devastating Question" concept—these are Socratic probes, not motivational pulls.
**Why it matters:** Questions force readers to internally answer before continuing. They create intellectual friction that makes doctrine stick. Too many sites answer questions for readers rather than asking questions OF readers. The Socratic approach is underutilized.
**Apply to:** All doctrinal and objection pages. Embed 2-4 genuine questions (not rhetorical) that readers must internally resolve.

### 2026-03-30 — ENHANCER (Session: Doctor-Corpse SEO Title Surgery) — CRAFT
**Discovery:** The pull-quote strategy works best when it IS the theological insight, not a restatement of the argument. Deployed: "The doctor does not ask the corpse for permission. He acts. And in acting, He creates the very capacity for gratitude that follows." This is the INVERSION—the surprising insight that explains everything backward. Many pages treat pull-quotes as motivational summaries. Best pull-quotes are theological reversals. They should make readers think "wait, that changes everything."
**Why it matters:** A pull-quote that merely repeats what was just explained is decoration. A pull-quote that INVERTS expectation is weaponized theology. The reader carries the pull-quote forward mentally because it subverted their intuition.
**Apply to:** All pages with pull-quotes. Ensure the pulled text is the SURPRISE, not the summary.

### 2026-03-30 — ENHANCER (Session: Evangelism Evangelism Objection Enhancement) — CRAFT
**Discovery:** The steelmanned objection section is a powerful rhetorical structure that strengthens rather than weakens apologetic pages. By presenting the strongest, most philosophically rigorous version of the opposing argument BEFORE refuting it, the page gains credibility. The reader thinks "they're not dodging the hard version" and becomes more receptive. The steelmanned objection should be 2-3 paragraphs of genuine philosophical force, then followed by "but here's why it fails." This structure is rare on Christian sites and immediately stands out.
**Why it matters:** Most objection pages either set up strawman versions or skip the objection's strongest form altogether. By naming the strongest version first, we demonstrate intellectual honesty and actually make the refutation more powerful. Readers know we're not avoiding the hard questions.
**Apply to:** All remaining objection pages. Every page answering "Does God...?" or "How can...?" should have a steelmanned objection section early on.
