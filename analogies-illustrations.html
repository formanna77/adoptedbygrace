Seeing Truth Through Metaphor

Analogies & Illustrations

Sometimes the best way to understand a truth is through a picture. These analogies make the invisible visible—turning abstract doctrine into unforgettable insight that lands in your soul.

Why Analogies Matter

Your mind grasps truth through pictures. A diagram unlocks what a paragraph couldn’t. That’s why Jesus didn’t argue about grace — He told stories. A good analogy bypasses intellectual walls and lands the truth where it needs to land: in the heart. These illustrations don’t replace Scripture — they illuminate it. They make the invisible visible. They turn abstract doctrine into lived experience. And sometimes, one perfect image is all it takes for the whole of God’s sovereignty to snap into focus.

🩺 Total Depravity & Regeneration

The Doctor and the Corpse

You can’t negotiate with the dead. What happens when you stop blaming the corpse for not healing itself—and start understanding what it means to be raised? This is the single most liberating realization about your condition and God’s power.

Ephesians 2:1–5 • John 5:21 → 🪦 Effectual Calling

Lazarus and the Grave

What does it look like when God doesn’t ask permission? When He doesn’t negotiate with death but commands it to release its prey? The Gospel isn’t an invitation you can refuse—it’s a resurrection you can’t survive.

John 11:43 • John 10:27–28 → ♟️ Sovereignty & Responsibility

The Chess Grandmaster

Everyone says God’s sovereignty and your freedom can’t both be true. What if they’re both true precisely because you’ve been thinking about them wrong? This is the image that finally lets you stop choosing between them.

Genesis 50:20 • Acts 2:23 → 💡 Conditions vs. Causes

The Light Switch

You flip a switch—but did you cause the light, or simply trigger what was already prepared? One distinction erases decades of guilt about your faith. One word changes everything: you’re the condition, not the cause.

Ephesians 2:8–9 • Philippians 1:29 → 📜 Election & Irresistible Grace

The Adoption

What if grace isn’t a door you open but a family that chose you before you knew your name? The papers are already signed. The security is not in your grip but in a love that will never let you go—no matter what you do.

Ephesians 1:4–5 • Romans 8:15 → 🌊 Total Depravity & Dead in Sin

The Drowning Man Who Thinks He’s Swimming

Every gospel presentation that says “you were drowning and God threw you a rope” has already conceded the wrong position. Scripture doesn’t say you were drowning. It says you were dead. And corpses don’t grab ropes.

Ephesians 2:1 • Ezekiel 37 • John 11:43 →
