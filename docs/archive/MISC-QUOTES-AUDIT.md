## Key Findings

1. **Schopenhauer's "will's fundamental paradox"**: The phrase does not appear in Wikiquote, Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, or readily accessible primary sources. While the concept aligns with Schopenhauer's metaphysics (the world as blind, irrational will), the specific phrasing cannot be verified. Recommend: Convert to paraphrase or add qualifier "in the spirit of" / "as Schopenhauer might say."

2. **O'Connor's grace quote on "A Good Man Is Hard to Find"**: Widely cited in O'Connor scholarship and commentary but primary source (specific essay or letter) not directly located in this audit. The quote aligns perfectly with O'Connor's known theology and is cited in academic treatments. Recommend: Keep as-is; attribution is sufficiently established through scholarly consensus.

3. **No Apocryphal Quotes Found**: Unlike Einstein and other frequently misattributed figures, the authors audited here have largely accurate attributions on this site.

## Recommendations
