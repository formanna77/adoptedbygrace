### Minor Issue Detected:

**File:** `secular-sociology-human-inability.html` + `anxious-mind-brain-decides.html`

**Claim:** "Fried et al. (2011) predicted decisions with 80% accuracy 700ms before awareness"

**Issue:** Fried et al. (2011) paper actually shows accuracy varies by analysis method (60-80% range depending on decoding algorithm). The site presents "80%" as a firm fact, but the actual paper hedges with "up to" or "around 60-80%."

**Recommendation:** Change to: "Fried et al. (2011) recorded individual neurons in motor cortex and achieved prediction accuracy of 60–80% up to 700ms before reported awareness."

### Critical Period Language Claim Assessment

The language acquisition claim (objection-why-command.html, line 297) is **factually correct** but attribution needs review:

**Claim verbatim:** "Neuroplasticity research at MIT has shown that infants deprived of spoken language in the first eighteen months of life sustain permanent linguistic damage."

**Verification:**
- ✅ Critical period for language acquisition: ~0-3 years (established by Lenneberg 1967)
- ✅ Deprivation → permanent deficits: Newport (1990), Mayberry & Lock (2003), case studies (Genie, Chelsea, Isabelle)
- ⚠️ "MIT has shown" — MIT linguists (Newport, et al.) did conduct critical-period research, but the specific claim is not exclusively an MIT finding

**Recommendation:** The claim is accurate. Attribution could say "Research on critical periods (Newport, Lenneberg) has established..." to be more precise about source, but current framing is acceptable since Newport is MIT-affiliated.
