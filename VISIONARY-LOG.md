## 2026-03-28 — Visionary Session #1

### Ideas Generated

1. **"The Algorithm Knows You Better Than You Know Yourself"** — A secular evidence page connecting the modern experience of algorithmic prediction (Netflix, Spotify, Amazon, Target, Facebook) to divine sovereignty. If a machine with incomplete data can predict your choices with 80-93% accuracy, the claim that an omniscient God can't know your future actions is absurd. Uses real research: Target pregnancy prediction, Facebook personality study (300 likes > spouse accuracy), 93% human predictability from MIT/Northeastern, Libet-adjacent keystroke dynamics. Pivots from unsettling to comforting: the algorithm knows you and monetizes you; God knows you and loves you anyway. **STATUS: BUILT**

2. **"Letters from the Adopted"** — A series of fictional first-person letters written by biblical figures AFTER they understand their own election. A letter from Paul looking back at Damascus: "I thought I was hunting heretics. He was hunting me." A letter from the thief on the cross: "I didn't find God in my final hour. He found me in His appointed one." A letter from Lazarus: "You can't choose to un-die yourself." Each letter explores the moment of realizing they didn't choose God — He chose them. Powerful because it's imaginative, not argumentative — it bypasses the intellectual defenses and lands in the heart. **STATUS: BACKLOG — Storyteller agent should build.**

3. **"The Parenting Proof"** — A page/devotional that uses the universal experience of parenting to illustrate irresistible grace. Every parent has grabbed a toddler's hand in a parking lot while the child screams "I want to do it myself!" The child experiences the grip as oppression. The parent knows it's salvation. The child's "free will" to run into traffic is precisely what the parent overrides — out of love. This maps perfectly to irresistible grace: God doesn't override your will against your good; He overrides your suicidal spiritual autonomy FOR your good. Extends to: parents choosing schools, doctors choosing surgery for unconscious patients, lifeguards overriding a drowning person's thrashing. **STATUS: BACKLOG — Builder or Storyteller.**

4. **"The Addiction Testimony"** — A page connecting the universal experience of addiction (not just substances — phones, approval, anger, control) to total depravity. Every person who has tried to stop a habit and failed knows experientially that the will is in bondage. AA's first step ("We admitted we were powerless") is the most Reformed sentence in secular culture. The page walks through: you know you should stop, you want to stop, you try to stop, you can't stop. That's Romans 7. That's total depravity. That's why you need a power outside yourself. The secular addiction recovery movement is an accidental confession of the bondage of the will. **STATUS: BACKLOG — Connects to psychology series.**

5. **"The 404 Page That Preaches"** — Redesign the 404 error page into a mini-evangelistic moment. When someone hits a dead link, instead of a generic error, they see: "You're lost. So were we all. (Ephesians 2:1-5)." Include a brief, beautiful 3-sentence message about being found by God when you didn't know you were lost, with links to Start Here and the "He Will Never Give Up On You" devotional. It's the kind of unexpected touch that makes people screenshot it and share it. **STATUS: BACKLOG — Quick win for any agent.**

## 2026-03-28 — Visionary Session #2

### Ideas Generated

1. **"The Orphanage"** — A long-form allegory where hundreds of children in an orphanage believe they choose their adoptive family on Adoption Day, until one girl named Mara finds the records room and discovers that the adoption papers were signed before the foundation of the orphanage — before any of the children existed. Maps Ephesians 1:4-5, John 15:16, Romans 9, and 1 John 4:19 through narrative, not argument. Features Mara (the elect discovering truth), Eli (the unlikely chosen one who can't believe he'd be wanted), and Kael (the one who rages against sovereignty because it dethrones the self). The story captures election, effectual calling, regeneration preceding faith, and the security of a love that begins in the Lover, not the beloved. **STATUS: BUILT**

2. **"Five Fears: What's Really Holding You Back from the Most Comforting Doctrine in the Bible"** — Takes the 5 most common *emotional* objections to sovereignty ("If God chose, He's a monster" / "My faith isn't real if I didn't choose" / "Predestination makes choices meaningless" / "A loving God would save everyone" / "Evangelism is pointless") and reframes each with a devastating analogy that flips the emotional argument. Not theological rebuttal — emotional revelation. Each "fear" gets a story-based answer that shows how the Reformed position is actually MORE loving, not less. **STATUS: BACKLOG — Apologetics/Pastoral crossover page.**

3. **"The Moment the Light Came On"** — A collection of fictional first-person vignettes capturing the moment someone first truly understood sovereign grace: the Arminian pastor who couldn't unsee Romans 9 after the hundredth reading, the recovering addict who knew experientially that his will was in bondage before a theologian said it, the grieving widow who found comfort ONLY in sovereignty, the new believer who was told "you chose God" but knew that wasn't what happened, the parent who watched their child ignore wisdom and suddenly understood why God doesn't just "offer" salvation. Mirrors the site's target audience. **STATUS: BACKLOG — Devotional/Stories crossover.**

4. **"What Your Mirror Can't Show You"** — Secular evidence page connecting Dunning-Kruger effect, the "better-than-average" bias (93% of drivers think they're above average), cognitive blind spots, and the neuroscience of self-deception to total depravity. Every human rates themselves as more moral than they are — which is EXACTLY what Jeremiah 17:9 says: "The heart is deceitful above all things." You literally cannot see your own depravity, and that inability to see it IS the depravity. Uses real published studies with citations. **STATUS: BACKLOG — Secular Evidence series.**

5. **"The Love Letter Dated Before Time"** — Ephesians 1:3-14 rewritten as intimate personal correspondence from God to the reader. Each theological phrase translated into first-person devotional language: "Before I made the mountains, I chose you. Before the first star burned, I loved you. Not because I foresaw something good in you — but because I decided to put something good in you." Paired with the actual Greek text alongside. Maps to the experience of finding a love letter written years ago that you only just discovered. **STATUS: BACKLOG — Devotional page, potentially most shareable content on the site.**

## 2026-03-29 — Visionary Session #3

### Ideas Generated

1. **"The Love Letter Dated Before Time"** — Ephesians 1:3-14 rewritten as intimate personal correspondence from God to the reader, framed as discovering a letter in your grandmother's attic dated before you were born. Each verse becomes a line of first-person devotional language: "Before I made the mountains, I chose you. Before the first star burned, I loved you. Not because I foresaw something good in you — but because I decided to put something good in you." Includes Greek deep-dive (exelexato, proorisas, esphragisthete) showing that every verb is active on God's part and passive on ours. The grandmother's-attic framing makes election viscerally personal before the theology even begins. **STATUS: BUILT**

2. **"The Gravity You Can't Escape"** — A secular-meets-devotional page using gravity as the physical metaphor for total depravity. You can't decide to stop being affected by gravity. You can jump, but you always come back down. You can build machines that temporarily defy it, but the moment the engine stops, you fall. The only way to truly escape earth's gravity is for a force GREATER than gravity to act upon you — a rocket with enough thrust. And you don't generate that thrust yourself. Someone else builds the rocket, fuels it, straps you in, and launches you. That's regeneration. That's grace. Maps perfectly to the self-help culture that tells people to "just try harder" spiritually — the equivalent of flapping your arms and calling it flight. **STATUS: BACKLOG — Analogies or Secular Evidence series.**

3. **"The Night You Were Named"** — A devotional meditation on the ancient practice of God naming people before they were born (Isaiah 49:1, Jeremiah 1:5, Galatians 1:15). The human hook: your parents named you before they met you face to face. They chose your name while you were still forming — unable to consent, unable to object, unable to earn it. Your name was their declaration of intent toward you. You didn't choose your name. Your name chose your story. The theological payoff: God knowing us before the foundation of the world, calling us by name (Isaiah 43:1), writing our names in the Lamb's book of life (Revelation 13:8). The name isn't a response to who you became. It's a prophecy of who He decided you would be. **STATUS: BACKLOG — Devotional page. Connects to "Love Letter" as a companion piece.**

4. **"The Courtroom Where You're Both Defendant and Evidence"** — An analogy page about a criminal trial where the defendant IS the evidence of their own guilt — they can't testify on their own behalf because everything they say proves the prosecution's case. Every defense you mount is another exhibit. The self-righteous person who says "I'm a good person" has just demonstrated pride. The moralist who says "I've kept the commandments" has just demonstrated self-reliance. The philosopher who says "I don't need God" has just demonstrated the exact condition Scripture diagnoses. The only hope is a defense attorney who doesn't put you on the stand but enters His own righteousness as a substitute verdict. Maps to Romans 3:10-20, the law as mirror, and imputed righteousness. **STATUS: BACKLOG — Analogies series.**

5. **"What Sleeping People Never Do"** — A devastatingly simple page built on one observation: sleeping people never wake themselves up. They can dream about waking up. They can sleep-talk about wanting to wake up. But the actual act of waking requires something OUTSIDE the sleep to break in — an alarm, a hand on the shoulder, sunlight through the window. This maps to Ephesians 2:1-5 and spiritual death. Every person has experienced being woken up by someone else. Nobody has experienced waking themselves from deep sleep by an act of will. That universal experience IS the doctrine of regeneration preceding faith — we just never connected the two. The page should be SHORT. Under 800 words. The power is in the simplicity. **STATUS: BACKLOG — Analogies series. Perfect quick-win.**

## 2026-03-30 — Visionary Session #4

### Ideas Generated

1. **"The Billion Decisions You Didn't Make Today"** — A secular evidence page that uses the reader's own body against the free will argument. Your heart beat 100,000 times yesterday. Your immune system destroyed thousands of abnormal cells. Your bone marrow manufactured 200 billion red blood cells. Your DNA repair mechanisms fixed 10,000-100,000 lesions. None of this asked your permission. The autonomic nervous system governs everything that keeps you alive — and you've never once complained about that sovereignty. The devastating question: if God didn't trust your heartbeat to your free will, why would He trust your salvation to it? Connects to Psalm 139:13-14 (sakak — knitting/weaving), Colossians 1:17 (sunesteken — holding together), and Ephesians 2:1-5 (dead, made alive). The humor kicker: "If your salvation depended on your free will the way your body depends on it, you'd be dead before breakfast." **STATUS: BUILT**

2. **"The Prodigal Who Thought He Walked Home"** — A re-reading of the Prodigal Son (Luke 15:11-32) that zooms in on the details everyone misses. The father was WATCHING — "while he was still a long way off." The father RAN. The father interrupted the rehearsed repentance speech. The son had a plan; the father had a better one. Every conversion testimony says "I decided to come back to God." But probe deeper: who put the hunger in the pig pen? Who made the far country feel empty? Who was watching the road? The son thinks he walked home. The Father was drawing him the entire time. This doesn't attack the human experience of returning — it DEEPENS it, showing God as the actor behind the actor. **STATUS: BACKLOG — Devotional/Stories crossover. Could be devastating as a short-form piece.**

3. **"The Anesthesia Argument"** — Before surgery, the anesthesiologist puts you under. You surrender consciousness, control, and will. You trust a stranger to cut you open because you trust the sovereign skill of the surgeon more than you trust your ability to perform surgery on yourself. Nobody calls this an insult to their dignity. Nobody demands to be awake and in charge during open-heart surgery. But when God says "I will perform the surgery of regeneration on your dead heart, and you will be passive in the process" — suddenly it's offensive. The page maps the entire surgical experience (diagnosis you didn't want, anesthesia you didn't fight, a procedure performed entirely by another's hands, a waking up to a new reality you didn't create) to the ordo salutis. The most Reformed thing any human being does in their life is go under general anesthesia. **STATUS: BACKLOG — Analogies series. Extraordinarily relatable.**

4. **"What Your Tears Already Know"** — A devotional exploring the phenomenon of involuntary tears. You don't decide to cry. The tears come — unbidden, unwanted, uncontrollable. The grieving widow doesn't choose her tears. The soldier at a funeral doesn't will his eyes to water. The person who hears a piece of music that reaches something they can't name doesn't consent to the emotion. Tears are the body's confession that you are not in charge of your deepest responses. This maps to conversion: the moment grace breaks through, it doesn't ask for a committee vote. It overwhelms. It breaks. It rebuilds. Acts 16:14 — "The Lord opened her heart." Lydia didn't schedule the opening. She wept because Someone had already begun the surgery. **STATUS: BACKLOG — Devotional. Beautiful companion piece to "Billion Decisions."**

5. **"The Trial of Free Will v. Romans 9"** — A long-form dramatic piece structured as an actual courtroom trial. Free Will is the defendant. Romans 9 is the prosecution. The jury is the reader. Opening statements, witness testimony (calling Paul, Moses, Pharaoh, Jacob, Esau to the stand), cross-examination, objections overruled, closing arguments. The twist: the defense attorney for Free Will keeps making arguments that Romans 9 has already anticipated and answered — "Is there injustice on God's part? By no means!" (v.14), "Why does he still find fault? Who are you, O man?" (v.19-20). The trial reveals that every objection to sovereignty is already addressed IN the text, as if Paul knew exactly what we would say. Because he did. Because the Spirit did. **STATUS: BACKLOG — Stories/Demolition crossover. Major project, potentially the most creative page on the site.**
