## Pattern Analysis

### What the Top Pages (8.5+) Have in Common — THE DNA OF DEVASTATION

1. **They name the reader's experience with startling precision BEFORE explaining doctrine.** resistance-last-idol describes the exact moment of existential crisis. Never-gives-up names the survival instinct that poisoned your relationship with God. This is the site's signature technique and the single biggest differentiator between 9+ pages and 6-7 pages.

2. **They build emotional momentum toward a revelation.** The best pages don't front-load doctrine — they build toward it. Dead City builds to the Voice. Last Idol builds to the joy of surrender. Never Gives Up builds from fear to rest. The reader is emotionally ready for the truth before it arrives.

3. **They have ONE controlling metaphor that carries from opening to close.** Dead City = grey city coming alive. Last Idol = throne room / idol. Never Gives Up = two hands of Father and Son. The weaker pages lack this through-line.

4. **Scripture enters at the moment of maximum impact.** Not as decoration, not as proof-texting, but at the exact moment the reader's heart is open and the verse will hit hardest.

5. **They end with worship or rest, not summary.** The best pages don't conclude with "In conclusion, we have seen that..." They end with Scripture speaking directly to the reader, or with a moment of wonder/worship.

### What the Bottom Pages (6-7) Lack

1. **Generic/academic openings.** question-romans9 and demolition-john3-16 open with the verse/text structure. No hook. No arresting first sentence. They feel like papers, not encounters.

2. **Emotional warmth and pastoral wonder.** The weakest pages don't worship. They explain. They're correct, but cold. A page about Romans 9 should make you weep or tremble — this one makes you take notes.

3. **Humor.** The weakest pages average 3.5/10 on humor. The strongest average 7.3/10. Zero correlation between humor and reduced seriousness — the funniest page (neuroscience) is also one of the most intellectually rigorous.

4. **The "so what for real life" bridge.** Demolition and question pages explain what the text says but don't connect it to the reader's Tuesday afternoon, their 2am fears, their actual lived experience of faith.

### Category Strength Rankings

1. **Psychology/Resistance** — Consistently 8.4-9.1. The Psychologist agent is producing the best content on the site. Its technique of "name the phenomenon → explain it scientifically → show Scripture predicted it" is the gold standard.

2. **Devotionals** — Strong (8.5). High emotional resonance, excellent openings, genuine worship. Could strengthen intellectual rigor slightly.

3. **Secular Evidence** — Surprisingly strong (8.3). The neuroscience page is world-class. Humor works brilliantly. The secular entry point disarms resistance.

4. **Stories** — Good (8.1) but only 5 stories exist. Massive room for growth. Allegories are transparent — doctrine visible too early.

5. **Homepage** — Strong (8.4). Excellent first impression. Missing humor entirely.

6. **Demolition/Question Pages** — THE WEAKEST CATEGORY (6.4-6.9). Intellectually rigorous but emotionally flat. This is the #1 quality gap on the site. These pages are where skeptics and Arminians will spend the most time — and they're the least likely to move a heart.

Two new pages were added since the inaugural session: **story-the-room-where-you-chose.html** and **secular-billion-decisions.html**. Both are outstanding.

**The Room Where You Chose** (9.3) is a breakthrough. It represents a new CATEGORY of content on the site: the *thought experiment that deploys narrative*. This is exactly the kind of hybrid form the 101 Ways Principle demands. It reaches people that neither the story pages nor the question pages reach: the person who needs to *trace the logic of sovereignty through their own life* to believe it.

**The Billion Decisions** (9.0) is the site's strongest secular evidence page. The controlling metaphor (your body's autonomic systems) is viscerally relatable. The line "God gave your will the fork. He kept the heartbeat for Himself" is a brilliant connection that will stay in readers' minds for years. The humor lands ("If your salvation depended on your free will the way your body depends on it, you'd be dead before breakfast"). Scripture enters at maximum impact moments. This page reaches the *science-minded skeptic* and the *person who responds to concrete physical evidence more than abstract theology*.

### THE BIG PATTERN: Literary Device Gap Is the #1 Opportunity

After evaluating 16 pages across two sessions, the pattern is unmistakable:

**Pages that deploy a SPECIFIC literary device with mastery score 8.5+. Pages that default to "standard exegesis article" score 6-7.**

The site's content falls into two tiers:
- **TIER 1 (8.5+):** Pages that use a clear, deliberate literary device — thought experiment (Room Where You Chose), personal essay/psychological analysis (Last Idol), pastoral address (Never Gives Up), secular-to-sacred bridge (Billion Decisions), historical narrative (Augustine-Pelagius)
- **TIER 2 (6-7):** Pages that default to "present the verse, explain the Greek, state the argument, answer objections" — the question-*.html and demolition-*.html pages

**This is not a content quality problem. It's a literary device problem.** The Tier 2 pages have STRONGER arguments than many Tier 1 pages. Romans 9's "objection proves the doctrine" trap is logically more devastating than anything in the Room story. But the story MOVES people while the exegesis page INFORMS them. The difference is the literary vessel, not the theological cargo.

## Agent Prompt Changes Made — 2026-03-30

### 1. Builder (abg-builder/SKILL.md) — LITERARY DEVICE ROTATION SYSTEM

**Problem:** Builder produces strong content but defaults to the same "verse → Greek → argument → objection → verdict" structure. 80% of question-*.html pages are structurally identical. This creates quantity without diversity.

**Change:** Add a mandatory Literary Device Rotation system. Builder must check which devices have been used recently and deploy a DIFFERENT one each session. The rotation includes at minimum: Socratic dialogue, first-person retelling, forensic/courtroom argument, comparative study, thought experiment, letter format, investigative journalism, "day in the life" narrative, reductio ad absurdum, and scientific hypothesis-test format.

**Also:** Add an explicit instruction that when building question-*.html pages, the Builder should consider whether the page would be MORE effective in a non-standard format. "Could this page about John 6 work better as a first-person narrative from the crowd's perspective? Could this page about Ephesians 2 work better as a letter from Paul to a modern reader?"

### 2. Storyteller (abg-storyteller/SKILL.md) — AUDIENCE TARGETING

**Problem:** Stories are excellent but tend toward the same implied reader: a thoughtful adult already somewhat familiar with theology. No stories target teenagers, children, grieving people, or people from non-Christian backgrounds.

**Change:** Add a mandatory "Target Reader" declaration at the top of each story. Before writing, the Storyteller must name WHO this story is for — not "everyone" but a specific person. Then add a cross-reference check: "Is there already a story for this type of reader? If yes, choose a different target."

### 3. Refiner — LITERARY DEVICE UPGRADE PATH

**Problem:** Refiner strengthens arguments and adds quotes, but never changes the STRUCTURE or LITERARY DEVICE of a page. A question page that would be 10x more effective as a courtroom drama stays as a standard exegesis article forever.

**Change:** Add a new first step: "Literary Device Assessment." Before touching content, the Refiner asks: "Is this page using the right literary device for its content? Would a different format make this page dramatically more effective?" If yes, the Refiner should note the recommendation in ROADMAP.md rather than doing a structural overhaul (which is Builder's job), but this surfaces the insight.

### 4. Wordsmith — HUMOR INJECTION PROTOCOL

**Problem:** Humor is still 5.6/10 site-wide — the lowest dimension by far. The Wordsmith's prompt mentions humor but doesn't provide enough guidance on HOW to inject it.

**Change:** Add a "Humor Toolkit" section with specific techniques: (1) The absurdist observation ("If your salvation depended on free will the way your body depends on it..."), (2) The theological wink (parenthetical asides that show the writer's personality), (3) The unexpected analogy that makes you laugh then think, (4) The gentle self-deprecating moment. Also add: "For every page you touch, add or strengthen at least one humor beat. The neuroscience page and billion-decisions page prove that humor INCREASES credibility rather than diminishing it."
