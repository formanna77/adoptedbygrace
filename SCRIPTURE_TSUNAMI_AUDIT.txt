L - LIMITED/DEFINITE ATONEMENT
──────────────────────────────
Total Verses:         95
Key Strength:         73
Supporting:           22
Weak Verses:          4 (4.21%)
Strong Verses:        91 (95.8%)

Status: STRONG with IDENTIFIABLE WEAKNESSES
This is the weakest category. Several verses use universal language ("world,"
"everyone") that requires commentary interpretation to harmonize with Limited
Atonement. These are not false verses—they're verses where the surface language
conflicts with the doctrine and needs explicitation.

Weak Verses:

  1. Hebrews 2:9 [supporting]
     Text: "But we see him who for a little while was made lower than the angels,
            namely Jesus, crowned with glory and honor because of the suffering of
            death, so that by the grace of God he might taste death for everyone."
     Weakness: "For everyone" language—universalists cite this directly against
               Limited Atonement. Commentary must clarify "everyone" means the
               elect. This is the single most-cited verse against the doctrine.

  2. John 1:29 [key]
     Text: "The next day John saw Jesus coming toward him and said, 'Look, the
            Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world!'"
     Weakness: "The world" in John often means "all of humanity" or "all people."
               Requires interpretation that "world" here means the elect redeemed
               from all nations, not universal atonement for all people.

  3. John 3:16 [key]
     Text: "For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that
            whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life."
     Weakness: "God loved the world" and "whoever believes"—explicitly universal
               language. This verse is the most-cited objection to election and
               Limited Atonement. Requires commentary interpretation that "world"
               means the elect and "whoever" is open to all but effectual only for
               the elect.

  4. Revelation 5:9 [key]
     Text: "And they sang a new song, saying, 'Worthy are you to take the scroll
            and to open its seals, for you were slain, and by your blood you
            ransomed people for God from every tribe and language and people and
            nation.'"
     Weakness: "From every tribe and language and people and nation" could be read
               as either: (a) proof of definite atonement (redeemed peoples from
               every group) or (b) weakness suggesting universalism (redemption
               touches all groups). The ambiguity leaves room for alternative
               interpretation.

KEY FINDINGS
============

1. TOTAL DEPRAVITY IS UNASSAILABLE (99.0% strength)
   The foundation doctrine is overwhelmingly supported. The single weak verse is
   thematic enhancement, not weakness of argument.

2. UNCONDITIONAL ELECTION IS UNASSAILABLE (99.0% strength)
   God's sovereign choice is clearly and repeatedly affirmed. No significant
   opposition verses included.

3. LIMITED ATONEMENT IS THE VULNERABLE POINT (95.8% strength)
   Four verses use universal language that requires interpretive work:
   - Hebrews 2:9 ("for everyone")
   - John 1:29 ("the world")
   - John 3:16 ("the world" / "whoever")
   - Revelation 5:9 ("every tribe")
   
   These are not weak in the sense of being "wrong" verses—they're included
   because their surface language conflicts with the doctrine. This creates a
   strategic vulnerability: visitors can point to these and say "your own Bible
   contradicts you" unless the commentary fully explains them. Consider:
   a) Whether all four should be included or if some should be replaced
   b) Whether the commentaries are sufficiently clear to defend the doctrine
   c) Whether this invites the criticism it's designed to answer

4. IRRESISTIBLE GRACE IS NEARLY UNASSAILABLE (98.9% strength)
   Only one weak verse (Acts 2:39), which is a minor issue of clarity rather than
   contradiction.

5. PERSEVERANCE IS NEARLY UNASSAILABLE (98.9% strength)
   The doctrine of security is powerfully affirmed across Scripture. The single
   weak verse uses conditional language that commentaries clarify.


RECOMMENDATIONS
===============

1. CONSIDER REPLACING JOHN 3:16 [L]
   Status: The most-cited objection verse in Christendom
   Issue: "God loved the world" and "whoever believes" are explicitly universal
          language
   Risk: Visitors expecting to see this verse contradicted will see it included
         and assume the site dodges the hardest texts
   Alternative: Replace with a verse that teaches Limited Atonement more clearly,
                such as John 17:9 ("I pray not for the world but for those you
                gave me") or Matthew 26:28 ("blood shed for many")

2. ENSURE COMMENTARIES ARE AIRTIGHT ON JOHN 1:29, HEBREWS 2:9
   These three verses (John 1:29, Hebrews 2:9, Revelation 5:9) are frequently
   cited by opponents. The commentaries MUST be:
   - Precise about what "world" and "everyone" mean in context
   - Clear that these don't teach universal atonement
   - Accessible to skeptics, not hidden behind jargon
   
   Current commentaries appear adequate but should be stress-tested against
   Arminian counterarguments.

3. LIMITED ATONEMENT NEEDS OFFENSIVE VERSES
   Consider whether the site should include verses that AFFIRMATIVELY teach
   Limited Atonement (not just answer objections):
   - John 10:11 ("lays down his life for the sheep")
   - John 17:9 ("not for the world but for those given me")
   - Titus 2:14 ("gave himself for us to redeem us")
   - Hebrews 9:28 ("offered to take away sins of many people")
   
   The current collection defends against universalism but might not
   affirmatively prove Limited Atonement without commentary.


OVERALL ASSESSMENT
==================

The Scripture Tsunami collection achieves a 98.3% strength rating—an exceptionally
high standard. Of 478 verses, only 8 lack clear, surface-level teaching of their
assigned doctrine. The collection successfully demonstrates:

✓ Total Depravity is biblically overwhelming
✓ Unconditional Election is explicitly taught
✓ Irresistible Grace is powerfully affirmed
✓ Perseverance of the Saints is robustly supported
✓ Limited Atonement requires explanation but can be defended from Scripture

The weakness in Limited Atonement is not a fatal flaw—it's an honest
acknowledgment that certain verses use universalist language. The question is
whether including these verses alongside commentary explanation is more
persuasive (showing the site doesn't dodge hard texts) or less persuasive (making
the doctrine appear weaker than necessary).

For a mission-critical resource intended to "destroy the insidious lie of
Arminianism," a 98.3% strength rating is excellent. The site can confidently
present these verses as the biblical basis for the doctrines of grace.
