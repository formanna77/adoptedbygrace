## The 8 Weak Verses

A verse is classified as "weak" if:
1. It uses language that appears to contradict the doctrine (requiring interpretation)
2. An informed skeptic could offer a plausible alternative reading without doing deep exegesis
3. It lacks explicit teaching of the assigned doctrine at the surface level

### Total Depravity (1 weak)
- **Ecclesiastes 7:29** - Describes corruption but not spiritual inability

### Unconditional Election (1 weak)
- **Matthew 13:16-17** - Blessing language without explicit election teaching

### Limited Atonement (4 weak) ⚠️
- **Hebrews 2:9** - "For everyone" (cited by universalists against LA)
- **John 1:29** - "Sin of the world" (requires interpretation)
- **John 3:16** - "God loved world" + "whoever believes" (most-cited objection)
- **Revelation 5:9** - "Every tribe/language/people/nation" (ambiguous)

### Irresistible Grace (1 weak)
- **Acts 2:39** - Calling language unclear on effectual vs external call

### Perseverance (1 weak)
- **Psalm 89:30-34** - Conditional "if" language suggests possible apostasy

## Key Insights

### What's Strong
- **Total Depravity & Unconditional Election**: Nearly perfect (99.0% each)
- The linchpin doctrines are overwhelmingly supported
- 305 "key strength" verses that are knockout proof

### What's Weak
- **Limited Atonement**: The weakest area (95.8%)
- Four verses use universal language requiring commentary to harmonize
- Three of these (John 1:29, John 3:16, Hebrews 2:9) are commonly cited objections
- This reflects an honest acknowledgment that certain biblical language is universalistic

### Strategic Consideration
The site includes verses with universalist language alongside commentaries explaining them. This is:
- **Strengths**: 
  - Doesn't dodge hard texts
  - Shows confidence in exegesis
  - Builds credibility with honest engagement
- **Weaknesses**: 
  - Gives opponents ammunition ("your own verses contradict you")
  - Requires reader engagement with commentary to see the point
  - May weaken perceived doctrine strength vs. having only exclusively-LA verses

## Recommendations

1. **Consider replacing John 3:16** - Most objection verse; could use one teaching LA more clearly like John 17:9
2. **Stress-test Limited Atonement commentaries** - Ensure they address Arminian counterarguments clearly
3. **Add more offensive LA verses** - Currently defensive; could include John 10:11, John 17:9, Matthew 26:28

## Conclusion

A 98.3% strength rating is exceptional for a theological resource. The Scripture Tsunami collection provides robust biblical foundation for all five doctrines of grace. The concentrated weakness in Limited Atonement is not fatal—it's an honest reflection that this doctrine requires more interpretive work to harmonize with certain biblical language than the other four points.

The site can confidently present this as the biblical basis for the doctrines of grace.
