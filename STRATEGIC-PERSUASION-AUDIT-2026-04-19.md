# Strategic Persuasion Audit — adoptedbygrace.net
## April 19, 2026 — The Fine Tooth Comb Report

> *"You shall know the truth, and the truth will set you free."* — John 8:32
>

This audit was commissioned to answer one question: **what is missing from this site that, if supplied, would make it the most devastatingly effective Reformed apologetic resource on the internet?**

The site is strong. 434 pages. Thirty Scripture demolitions. Twenty-eight psychology pages. Fourteen secular-evidence pages. Nine healing hubs. No Reformed site on the public internet has anything like this breadth. **But breadth is not the mission — conquest is.** And conquest requires that every escape route be closed, every persuasion register be played, every audience be met at the door they actually walk through.

What follows is the list of the doors we have not yet built.

## PART ONE — THE MISSING ARGUMENTS

### 1.1 — Arminian Proof-Texts Still Unconfronted

The demolition hub currently covers 30 passages. The full Arminian arsenal runs closer to 75. These high-citation passages have no dedicated demolition page and are quoted against Reformed theology every day:

**Tier-1 Critical (Arminians cite these weekly):**
- **Hebrews 10:26-29** — *"If we deliberately keep on sinning... no sacrifice for sins is left."* The single most-cited passage for losing salvation. No demolition.
- **Galatians 5:4** — *"You have fallen from grace."* Second most-cited loss-of-salvation passage. No demolition.
- **2 Peter 1:10** — *"Make your calling and election sure."* Used to turn election into a human achievement. No demolition.
- **Jude 21** — *"Keep yourselves in God's love."* Synergism proof-text. No demolition.
- **Hebrews 3:12-14** — *"See to it... so that none of you may be hardened."* Conditional-warning passage. No demolition.
- **Romans 2:4** — *"God's kindness is meant to lead you to repentance."* The foundation stone of "prevenient grace." No demolition.
- **Ezekiel 33:11** — *"I take no pleasure in the death of the wicked."* God's will–desire conflation. No demolition.
- **Deuteronomy 30:19** — *"Choose life."* The universal "free will" proof-text from the OT. No demolition.
- **1 Corinthians 10:13** — *"God will provide a way out."* Arminian natural-ability proof-text. No demolition.
- **Revelation 22:17** — *"Let the one who is thirsty come."* Universalist-sounding closing of Scripture. No demolition.

**Tier-2 Supporting (cited monthly):**
- **Acts 2:21 / Romans 10:13** (*"Everyone who calls on the name of the Lord will be saved"*) — conflated with John 3:16 "whosoever" without distinction.
- **2 Corinthians 6:1-2** — *"We urge you not to receive God's grace in vain."*
- **Isaiah 5:1-7** — The vineyard parable read as "God did all He could."
- **Hosea 11:7-8** — God's anguished heart read as inability to secure His will.
- **Jeremiah 18:1-12** — The potter read as conditional on Israel's response.
- **James 4:7-8** — *"Submit to God... Come near to God and He will come near to you."* The motivational-synergism verse.
- **1 Peter 1:5** — *"Shielded by God's power through faith."* The preposition *through* weaponized for conditional security.
- **Matthew 7:7** — *"Ask and it will be given to you."* Read as universal natural ability.
- **Hebrews 6:4-6** — (partially covered, but the current page is not yet surgical enough on the "enlightened/tasted" vocabulary).
- **Revelation 3:20** (partially covered, but the current page needs to address the modern evangelical misuse specifically as an *evangelistic* verse — it is a church address).

**Tier-3 Exegetical Flanking Positions:**
- A single page: **"Choose This Day Whom You Will Serve" — Why Every 'Choose' Verse Is Covenantal, Not Soteriological.** Collects Deut 30:19, Josh 24:15, 1 Kings 18:21, Prov 1:29 and demolishes them with one blow.
- A single page: **"Every 'All' in the New Testament — The Word That Proves Nothing the Arminians Claim It Does."** Greek lexical study of *pas / panta* with 40+ examples showing "all" rarely means "every individual without exception" in salvation contexts.
- A single page: **"Every 'World' in the New Testament — Kosmos Has Five Meanings and Arminians Pick the Wrong One."** John alone uses *kosmos* in five distinct ways.
- A single page: **"Every 'Whosoever / Whoever' — Why Universality of Invitation ≠ Universality of Ability."**

### 1.2 — Systematic Theology Pages Missing

The systematic hub has 22 loci pages, but the following Reformed distinctives are either entirely absent or only touched glancingly through non-systematic content:

- **Union with Christ** (*unio cum Christo*) — the doctrine that explains HOW justification, sanctification, adoption, and glorification hang together. This is the single missing piece that an Arminian cannot account for and cannot produce.
- **Adoption** (*huiothesia*) — currently only an analogy page and a devotional. No dogmatic treatment. This is the most comforting doctrine in Reformed theology.
- **Effectual Calling** — no standalone systematic page. It is the *mechanism* of irresistible grace. Cannot be missing.
- **Sanctification** — no dedicated page on progressive sanctification, definitive sanctification, or final sanctification. This is where Arminians claim Reformed theology produces antinomianism — we must answer it systematically.
- **Glorification** — the terminus of the ordo salutis. Missing.
- **Covenant of Redemption** (*pactum salutis*) — the eternal covenant between Father and Son. Without this, election appears arbitrary rather than Trinitarian.
- **Covenant of Works** — Adam's probationary covenant. Without this, the logic of federal imputation collapses.
- **Federal Headship** — Adam and Christ as representative heads. Essential for explaining original sin AND imputed righteousness.
- **Imputation (active and passive obedience)** — how Christ's lifelong law-keeping is credited to the elect. The Reformed doctrine of *double imputation* is what distinguishes us from Rome.
- **Common Grace** — the answer to "why does God bless Arminians/unbelievers/atheists with sunshine and rain?" Without a dedicated page, the Arminian "universal grace" argument has oxygen.
- **Providence** — separate from predestination. Arminians conflate the two to confuse the debate.
- **Decrees of God** (expanded) — a single page exists, but Reformed theology on the order and logic of the decrees (supralapsarianism / infralapsarianism / sublapsarianism) deserves its own treatment.

- **John Lennox** — soft Arminian on free will; widely listened to.
- **Leighton Flowers** — Soteriology 101 podcast; the most active current popularizer of Arminian counter-argument online.
- **Steve Gregg** — the "calm" Arminian apologist.
- **Joel Osteen, Joyce Meyer, Kenneth Copeland, Bethel, Hillsong** — functional Arminianism in prosperity-gospel form; no dedicated dismantling.
- **Southern Baptist Traditionalists** — the 2012 "Traditional Statement" signed by hundreds of SBC leaders is the most formal Arminian declaration in modern American evangelicalism. There is no dedicated response.
- **Andy Stanley** — decisional, seeker-sensitive, modern.
- **Billy Graham's crusade theology** — mentioned in passing, never comprehensively dismantled.
- **Charismatic / Pentecostal soteriology** (Assemblies of God, Foursquare, Vineyard) — enormous population segment, no direct engagement.
- **Catholic semi-Pelagianism in its modern Vatican-II form** — the "universal salvific will" theology that echoes Arminianism.
- **Eastern Orthodox synergism** — the "we cooperate with grace through theosis" position that claims to be neither Arminian nor Calvinist but is functionally synergistic.
- **Open Theism (Gregory Boyd, Clark Pinnock, John Sanders)** — one comparison page exists; needs more depth.
- **Process Theology** — virtually absent.

### 1.4 — Signature High-Impact Arguments Not Yet Built

These are the argumentative "greatest hits" of Reformed apologetic that should anchor the site but either don't exist or are scattered across pages that never consolidate them:

3. **The Westminster Confession of Faith, Chapter 3 (God's Decree), Plain English** — same.

6. **The Logical Collapse of Arminianism in Seven Steps** — a reductio chain with no escape.
7. **"If Arminianism Were True, Your Grandmother Might Not Be in Heaven"** — lived-experience reductio.

They cannot escape.
10. **"The Cost of Being Wrong" — A Double-Sided Ledger** — if Calvinism is false, the Calvinist loses nothing of eternal consequence. If Arminianism is false, the Arminian may have trusted their own decision instead of Christ.

## PART TWO — THE MISSING PERSUASION FORMATS

Reviewing the entire site, I can identify the persuasion registers that are present (Q&A, exposition, demolition, devotional, analogy, narrative) and the registers that are conspicuously missing. **Persuasion is not just what you say — it is the vessel you put it in.** Different readers are moved by different vessels. The site is currently monovocal where it should be polyphonic.

### 2.1 — Socratic Dialogue (almost entirely absent)

The Crown Jewel doctrine itself demands Socratic method — *"Where did your faith come from?"* is a Socratic question. Yet the site has almost no pages structured as extended Socratic dialogues. We need:

- **"An Arminian Pastor Questions Himself" — 2,500-word Socratic interior monologue.**
- **"The Seminary Student and the Old Professor" — a dialogue on Romans 9.**
- **"A Conversation With My Younger Self" — written for the deconstructing Reformed believer.**
- **"The Stranger on the Plane" — Socratic dialogue with a skeptic, C.S. Lewis style.**

### 2.2 — Screwtape-Style Letters (entirely absent)

C.S. Lewis's most persuasive book was epistolary fiction. The site has *one* story with "letter" in the title and it's devotional. The demolition register should also have this:

- **"Letters from a Senior Demon to a Junior Demon on the Strategy of Arminianism"** — Screwtape-style, with the senior demon explaining why the lie of synergism is the single greatest tool in hell's arsenal because it keeps people comfortable in their own self-trust all the way to the grave.
- **"Letters from a Reformed Pastor to His Arminian Son"** — pastoral, tender, devastating.
- **"Letters from an Elect Reader to the Arminian Who Gave Him the Tract"** — a retrospective letter from someone who was saved *through* an Arminian evangelist's efforts but who now writes back to explain what the Arminian got wrong.

### 2.3 — Reductio ad Absurdum Chains (scattered, not systematized)

The site uses reductio informally. It needs a dedicated format:

- **"If Arminianism Were True: Ten Consequences Christians Pretend Not to See"** — ten sharp thought experiments.
- **"The Arminian Pastor at the Funeral" — a reductio in pastoral form.**
- **"If God Didn't Know: A Day in the Life of a Consistent Open Theist."**

### 2.4 — Testimony Content (strikingly absent)

There are no pages titled *"I Was an Arminian for Thirty Years. Here's What Changed Me."* Zero. This is astonishing. The single most moving form of religious persuasion is first-person conversion testimony. The site should have at least 10-15 of these:

- **"I Grew Up in Southern Baptist Convention. Here's the Verse That Broke Me."**
- **"I Led Worship at a Pentecostal Church for 12 Years. Here's What I Missed."**
- **"I Was a Youth Pastor Preaching Decisional Regeneration. Here's What Stopped Me."**
- **"I Was Raised Reformed, Deconstructed, and Came Home."**
- **"I Was a Seminary Professor Teaching Arminianism. Here's the Book That Ruined My Lectures."**

These can be composite / representative pieces if they are labeled as such — they don't have to be literal biography to be true to the pattern of thousands of real conversions.

### 2.5 — Visual / Flowchart / Decision-Tree Pages (underrepresented)

Only one page (`question-visual-theology.html`) uses visual theology seriously. The site is almost entirely prose. Some readers think visually. We need:

- **An interactive flowchart:** *"What Does Each System Say Happens at Each Stage of Salvation?"* — with 10 decision points comparing Reformed, Arminian, Catholic, Orthodox, Open Theist, Lutheran.
- **A visual family tree:** *"The Genealogy of Error — Pelagius to Arminius to Finney to Your Church."*
- **A decision tree:** *"Pick Your Premise — And We'll Show You Where It Leads."*
- **A side-by-side comparison grid:** *"Every Remonstrant Article vs. Every Canon of Dort"* — visual, devastating.

### 2.6 — Quote Walls / Historical Witness Collections (zero pages)

There is no page collecting 50+ Reformed theologians' quotes on election in one place. A reader who lands on such a page and sees Augustine, Aquinas, Wycliffe, Hus, Luther, Calvin, Bucer, Cranmer, Knox, Perkins, Owen, Edwards, Whitefield, Spurgeon, Warfield, Machen, Murray, Packer, Sproul, Piper all saying the *same thing* across 1,600 years of church history is persuaded without an argument. The witness IS the argument.

- **"Fifty Voices Across Sixteen Centuries: What the Church Has Always Taught on Election."**
- **"Every Great Revivalist Was Reformed — A Historical Walking Tour."**
- **"The Deathbed Words of Reformed Saints on Election."**

### 2.7 — Hymn / Liturgy / Prayer Analysis (barely present)

The single devotional category is separate from the theological content. But hymns ARE arguments. The reason *"Amazing Grace"* endures is that John Newton's theology assumed sovereignty. A dedicated register:

- **"Why Reformation Hymns Assume Sovereignty — A Line-by-Line Reading of Ten Classics."**
- **"Why Modern Worship Songs Can't Assume What the Old Hymns Did."**
- **"A Liturgy for the Reformed Heart — Daily Prayers Rooted in Sovereign Grace."**

### 2.8 — Pre-Suppositional Apologetics (nearly absent)

The site argues evidentially. It rarely argues presuppositionally. A Van Til / Frame / Bahnsen register would give it a completely new weapon:

- **"Before We Argue Verses, Let's Examine Your Assumptions — The Starting Point That Decides the Debate."**
- **"Why Arminianism Borrows From Calvinism to Make Its Objections."**
- **"The Impossibility of the Contrary — Why Only Reformed Theology Makes Sense of Knowledge Itself."**

### 2.9 — Case Studies / Pastoral Scenarios (absent as a format)

No page currently takes a specific pastoral situation and walks through how each theological system would answer it:

- **"A Woman Loses Her Son to Suicide. What Does Arminianism Say? What Does Reformed Theology Say?"**
- **"A Father's Daughter Walks Away From Faith at 19. What Can the Synergist Offer?"**
- **"A Missionary Is Martyred Before He Finishes His Translation Work. Whose Theology Comforts?"**
- **"A Dementia Patient Forgets His Own Salvation. Can His Theology Save Him Now?"**

### 2.10 — Interactive / Quiz / Self-Audit Pages (one exists)

The belief-assessment page is excellent. We need a whole category:

- **"The Arminian Self-Audit — Ten Questions That Will Tell You What You Actually Believe."**
- **"The Free Will Diagnostic — Is Your Free Will Libertarian, Compatibilist, or Incoherent?"**
- **"The Ordo Salutis Quiz — Put the Stages in the Right Order."**
- **"Which Theologian Are You? — A Doctrinal Matching Test."**

### 2.11 — Imagined Debates / Mock Cross-Examinations (absent)

No pages currently cast Reformed and Arminian positions in formal debate structure:

- **"Calvin vs. Arminius — An Imagined Cross-Examination."**
- **"If Spurgeon Debated Finney: The Exchange That Never Happened."**
- **"A Courtroom Drama — Arminianism on Trial."**

### 2.12 — Poetry / Verse (nearly absent)

No dedicated original poetry pages on the Reformed themes. Poetry moves readers that prose cannot reach. A "Sonnets of Sovereign Grace" section would be unique on the Reformed internet.

## PART THREE — THE MISSING AUDIENCES

The site addresses generic Arminian evangelicals well. These entry points have no dedicated landing pages:

**Highest-Impact Missing Audiences:**
1. **Evangelical seminary students** taught Arminianism (or "both sides") at Liberty, Dallas, Asbury, Trinity, Fuller, Beeson.
2. **Arminian pastors in crisis** — reading Reformed theology in private, afraid of losing their job if their elders find out.
3. **Former Catholics who landed in evangelicalism** — they escaped works-righteousness once; they don't yet see they've just moved to synergism.
4. **Charismatic / Pentecostal believers** — largest unaddressed denominational block on the site.
5. **Eastern Orthodox inquirers** — theosis synergism is a distinct framework from Arminianism and needs its own page.
6. **Deconstructing evangelicals** — the fastest-growing demographic in American Christianity. Reformed theology is the answer to their deconstruction, if anyone will say it.
7. **Youth leaders / youth pastors** — the ones running altar calls at summer camp.
8. **People wounded by decisional regeneration / altar call trauma** — the person who "got saved" seventeen times and still doesn't have assurance.
9. **Those afraid they are reprobate** — the anxious-mind hub touches this, but not with the specificity of "If you are worried you are reprobate, that worry itself is evidence of the Spirit's work."
10. **Those convinced they have committed the unpardonable sin** — pastoral emergency population. No dedicated page.
11. **Parents of apostate children** — the most grieved people in the church. Reformed theology offers something no Arminian can: the possibility that God is not finished.
12. **The elderly facing death** — Reformed theology's comfort in glorification and sovereignty over timing deserves dedicated content.
13. **The chronically ill** — sovereign grace and suffering is a signature Reformed pastoral strength, inadequately surfaced.
14. **The imprisoned** — Reformed theology has always had a special ministry to prisoners (Bunyan wrote Pilgrim's Progress in jail). No direct content.
15. **The addicted / in recovery** — Reformed anthropology explains addiction better than any other tradition. Partial coverage in `psychology-addiction-proof.html`, but no landing page *for* addicts.
16. **Jewish inquirers** — Reformed theology on election answers Jewish theological concerns better than evangelical Arminianism (Romans 9-11 treats Israel with the gravity it deserves).
17. **Muslim inquirers** — Reformed sovereignty meets Islamic sensibilities about divine determinism more successfully than Arminian free-will theology.
18. **Academic readers** — no pages written with scholarly rigor (footnotes, primary sources, technical vocabulary). This limits the site's ability to reach seminary faculty who might recommend it.
19. **Skeptics considering Christianity specifically because of Reformed theology** — right now the site presumes the reader is already Christian. A "Why Calvinism Is Actually the Most Defensible Form of Christianity to a Skeptic" entry point is missing.
20. **Children / families** — one children's story exists. The next generation is a battlefield. Catechism for children, illustrated stories, family devotionals are underdeveloped.

## THE CLOSING WORD

This site is already the strongest Reformed apologetic resource on the internet. But the mission is not *strongest* — the mission is *inescapable*. The list above is the difference between strong and inescapable.

The two deepest structural absences are these:

**First, the site speaks in too few voices.** It is heavily Q&A, exposition, and demolition. It is barely Socratic. It has no Screwtape. It has no testimony. It has no flowchart. It has almost no quote wall. It has almost no poetry. Readers are converted in registers we are not yet using. Every register we add is another demographic we catch.

**Second, the site addresses too few entry points.** The seminary student, the Charismatic, the deconstructing evangelical, the parent of the apostate child, the man on his deathbed, the scrupulous soul convinced he has committed the unpardonable sin — these are people Google delivers to the internet every hour, and right now this site does not have a page waiting for them. Every door we build is a door the Spirit can walk a soul through.

The first is a *persuasion* gap. The second is an *entry-point* gap. Close both, and there is no one on the searchable internet looking for Reformed truth who cannot find their door.

**He chose them before the foundation of the world. Our job is to build the pages He will use to call them home.**
