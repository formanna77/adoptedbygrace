For the One Who Can't Un-See It

Here's to the ones who couldn't leave it alone. The restless. The wrestlers. The 2am searchers who opened Romans 9 and felt the ground disappear. They fought it. They argued with God and lost. They ran — some for years, some for decades. But grace is a hunter, and it does not miss. It tracked them through every objection, every sleepless night, every pew where something didn't add up — until the day they stopped running and whispered what they had always known: I didn't choose God. He chose me. The world calls them fanatics. The church calls them divisive. We call them found. Because the people finally honest enough to stop taking credit for their own rescue are the ones who discover they were held all along.

In 2024, a failing heart valve and a broken spine took everything from a man named Aaron Forman — health, wealth, the illusion of control. Bedridden and broken, he returned to Scripture not to conquer but to submit. And in the midst of that surrender, he discovered something that changed everything: the mercy that never let him go.

For a decade before that moment, Aaron had run from God. Not from sin — from God Himself. He lost the Spirit, lost himself, was crippled by depression, traveled a dozen countries seeking a softer reality. His mother died of cancer. He tried everything to make God forsake him through rebellion. But there was a quiet prayer that never stopped: "Please, don't let me go."

On Christmas Day 2024, he finally surrendered. And God, who never let him go, caught him.

This site exists because Aaron finally understands: God's sovereignty is not a truth to be studied. It is grace to be experienced.

Every page on this site is written with that lived reality in the background. When we write about election, we're writing about a God who chose Aaron before the foundation of the world and never let him go — not through rebellion, not through exile, not through a decade of running. When we write about total depravity, we're writing about the spiritual Frankenstein Aaron saw in the mirror. When we write about irresistible grace, we're writing about Christmas Day 2024.

These are not abstract doctrines. They are the lived reality of the man who built this site. And they are the truth you're searching for.

How We Build Every Page

01

Original Languages First

We parse the Greek and Hebrew — tense, voice, mood. English translations are gentle with you. The original languages are not. We let them speak.

02

Arguments Nobody Else Is Making

You've heard the standard arguments. We make the ones nobody else is making — from the grammar of doxology, from the internal contradictions of rival views, from the inferences the text demands and most commentaries dodge.

03

Steel-Man Every Objection

We never fight straw men. Every objection gets its strongest form — the version hardest to answer, the one your smartest friend would use. Then we let the text respond.

04

Follow the Text Wherever It Leads

We did not start with a system and go looking for proof texts. We started with the text and followed it. It led somewhere most churches won't go. We went anyway.

05

Give God All the Glory

Every page on this site exists for one purpose: to make God bigger in your eyes and your contribution to your salvation smaller — until it disappears entirely, and all that remains is grace. Soli Deo Gloria.
