Sermon Library · Faithful Preaching

Hear the Word Proclaimed

The best sermons on the doctrines of grace from the most faithful preachers — organized by topic and passage so you can hear the text preached well.

"How then will they call on him in whom they have not believed? And how are they to believe in him of whom they have never heard? And how are they to hear without someone preaching?" — Romans 10:14 (ESV)

Sovereignty & Election

Sermons on God's sovereign choice, unconditional election, and predestination.

Chosen by God (Teaching Series)
R.C. Sproul Ephesians 1; Romans 9 Ligonier Ministries
Sproul's landmark teaching series walking through the biblical case for unconditional election. Covers free will, the bondage of the will, double predestination, and the pastoral implications. Perhaps the finest audio/video introduction to the doctrines of grace available.
Listen at Ligonier →
The Doctrines of Grace in John (Sermon Series)
John MacArthur John 6:35-65 Grace to You
MacArthur's verse-by-verse exposition of John 6 — the Father's drawing, the Son's receiving, and the Spirit's work. Methodical, exegetical, and uncompromising on the text's meaning.
Listen at Grace to You →
The Absolute Sovereignty of God
Voddie Baucham Romans 9:1-29
Baucham preaches Romans 9 with characteristic directness and passion. He refuses to let the text be domesticated by human comfort and shows why Paul's argument demands unconditional election.
Unconditional Election
John Piper Romans 9:6-13 Desiring God
Part of Piper's series on Romans. A careful exposition of Jacob and Esau showing that God's purpose of election stands "not because of works but because of him who calls."
Listen at Desiring God →
Jacob I Loved, Esau I Hated
Steven Lawson Romans 9:10-13
Lawson's characteristically precise exposition of the most uncomfortable verses in Romans 9. He demonstrates that God's sovereignty in election is not arbitrary but purposeful, wise, and just.

Total Depravity

Sermons on man's fallen condition, spiritual inability, and the bondage of the will.

Dead in Sin (Radical Corruption)
R.C. Sproul Ephesians 2:1-3 Ligonier Ministries
Sproul unpacks the meaning of spiritual death — not merely sickness, but death. He explains why dead men cannot cooperate in their own resurrection and why grace must be the sole cause of salvation.
Listen at Ligonier →
The Total Depravity of Man
John MacArthur Romans 3:10-18 Grace to You
MacArthur walks through Paul's devastating indictment of the human race in Romans 3, showing that every faculty — mind, will, emotions, desires — is corrupted by the fall.
Listen at Grace to You →
Are People Basically Good?
Voddie Baucham Genesis 6:5; Jeremiah 17:9
Baucham confronts the culture's most comforting lie with the Bible's most sobering diagnosis. A powerful sermon on the depth of human depravity and the impossibility of self-salvation.

Irresistible Grace & Effectual Calling

Sermons on God's effectual call that unfailingly brings His elect to saving faith.

Irresistible Grace (Teaching Series)
R.C. Sproul John 6:37-44 Ligonier Ministries
Sproul explains the distinction between the outward call of the gospel and the inward, effectual call of the Holy Spirit. When God calls effectually, the result is certain — the dead are made alive.
Listen at Ligonier →
The Effectual Call
Steven Lawson Romans 8:29-30
Lawson traces the golden chain of redemption and shows that calling is not a mere invitation but a sovereign act of God that unfailingly produces faith, justification, and glorification.
New Birth
John Piper John 3:1-8; 1 Peter 1:3 Desiring God
Piper expounds Jesus' teaching to Nicodemus — the wind blows where it wishes. Regeneration is a sovereign act of God that precedes and produces faith. You must be born again, and you cannot birth yourself.
Listen at Desiring God →

Definite Atonement

Sermons on the particular, effective, saving work of Christ for His sheep.

Limited Atonement
R.C. Sproul John 10:11, 14-15 Ligonier Ministries
Sproul reframes the question: the issue is not the power of the atonement (which is infinite) but its design. Did Christ intend to save all people, or did He lay down His life for His sheep?
Listen at Ligonier →
For Whom Did Christ Die?
John MacArthur John 10:1-30 Grace to You
MacArthur expounds the Good Shepherd discourse and shows that Christ's atoning death was intentional, particular, and effective — He lays down His life for the sheep, and the sheep alone are given by the Father.
Listen at Grace to You →
The Death of Death in the Death of Christ
Steven Lawson Isaiah 53:4-12; Hebrews 9:12
Lawson presents the biblical and logical case for definite atonement — if Christ bore the sins of all without exception, then either all are saved or Christ's sacrifice fails for some. Neither option is biblical.

Perseverance of the Saints

Sermons on the eternal security of believers grounded in God's sovereign keeping power.

Perseverance of the Saints (Teaching Series)
R.C. Sproul Romans 8:28-39; John 10:27-30 Ligonier Ministries
Sproul distinguishes Reformed perseverance from the shallow "once saved, always saved" of easy-believism. True believers persevere because God preserves — and He loses none of His own.
Listen at Ligonier →
The Perseverance of the Saints
John Piper Romans 8:28-39 Desiring God
Piper walks through the golden chain of Romans 8:29-30 and the triumphant crescendo of 8:35-39. Nothing can separate us from the love of God because the chain has no broken links.
Listen at Desiring God →
Eternal Security
John MacArthur John 10:27-30 Grace to You
MacArthur shows that eternal security is not a license to sin but the most powerful assurance in the universe — grounded in Christ's promise that no one can snatch His sheep from His hand or the Father's.
Listen at Grace to You →

Trusted Preachers

These men have faithfully proclaimed the doctrines of grace for decades. Their full sermon archives are among the richest theological resources available.

R.C. Sproul
1939–2017
Founder of Ligonier Ministries. The great Reformed teacher of our generation. His gift was making complex theology accessible without dumbing it down.
Browse at Ligonier →
John Piper
b. 1946
Founder of Desiring God. Pastor, theologian, and author. His preaching burns with a vision of God's sovereignty as the foundation of all joy.
Browse at Desiring God →
John MacArthur
b. 1939
Pastor of Grace Community Church. 55+ years of verse-by-verse exposition. His commitment to the text has produced one of the largest sermon archives in history.
Browse at Grace to You →
Steven Lawson
b. 1951
President of OnePassion Ministries. One of the clearest, most precise expositors of Reformed soteriology alive today. His series on the doctrines of grace are indispensable.
Browse at Ligonier →
Voddie Baucham
b. 1969
Dean of Theology at African Christian University. A powerful preacher who brings fire, humor, and unflinching commitment to the sovereignty of God in every sermon.
Joel Beeke
b. 1952
President of Puritan Reformed Theological Seminary. A scholar-pastor who brings the depth of Puritan theology to modern preaching. Especially strong on the experiential side of Reformed piety.
