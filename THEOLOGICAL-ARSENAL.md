I ask, Is this unbelief a sin, or is it not? If it be, then Christ suffered the punishment due unto it, or He did not. ## SECTION 2: GREEK WORD STUDIES

### ἐκλέγομαι (eklegomai) — "to choose out, select, elect"
- **Parsing:** Middle voice of ἐκλέγω — the middle voice emphasizes God choosing FOR HIMSELF
- **Key verses:** Ephesians 1:4 (ἐξελέξατο — "He chose"), John 15:16 ("You did not choose me, but I chose you"), Acts 13:17, 1 Corinthians 1:27-28
- **Theological significance:** God's election is active, sovereign, and personal. The middle voice means God chose us for His own purposes and pleasure.

### τάσσω (tassō) — "to appoint, ordain, arrange"
- **Key verse:** Acts 13:48 — "as many as were APPOINTED (τεταγμένοι — perfect passive participle) to eternal life believed"
- **Theological significance:** The appointment precedes and causes the believing. Perfect tense = completed action with ongoing results. Passive voice = God is the one who appointed them.

### ἕλκω (helkō) — "to draw, drag"
- **Key verses:** John 6:44 ("No one can come to me unless the Father who sent me DRAWS him"), John 12:32
- **Theological significance:** This is the same word used in John 21:11 for DRAGGING a net full of fish, and Acts 16:19 for DRAGGING Paul and Silas. This is not a gentle wooing — it is an irresistible, effectual drawing.

### δίδωμι (didōmi) — "to give"
- **Key verses:** John 6:37 ("All that the Father GIVES me will come to me"), John 6:39, John 10:29, John 17:2, 6, 9, 24
- **Theological significance:** The elect are a GIFT from the Father to the Son. The giving precedes the coming. And Jesus will lose NONE of what the Father has given.

### χαρίζομαι (charizomai) — "to graciously give, to freely bestow"
- **Key verse:** Philippians 1:29 — "For it has been GRANTED (ἐχαρίσθη) to you that for the sake of Christ you should not only believe in him but also suffer"
- **Theological significance:** Even FAITH ITSELF is a gift graciously bestowed. The same root as χάρις (grace).

### γεννάω (gennaō) — "to beget, to give birth to"
- **Key verses:** John 1:13 ("born NOT of the will of man, but of God"), John 3:3-8, 1 John 5:1
- **Theological significance:** Regeneration is a BIRTH — passive, not chosen by the one being born. 1 John 5:1 in Greek: "Everyone who BELIEVES (present tense) has been BORN (perfect tense — completed prior action) of God." The new birth PRECEDES faith.

### προορίζω (proorizō) — "to predestine, to determine beforehand"
- **Key verses:** Romans 8:29-30, Ephesians 1:5, 11, Acts 4:28
- **Theological significance:** The prefix πρό (before) + ὁρίζω (to set boundaries, determine). God set the boundaries of salvation BEFORE time. Same root as "horizon" — God determined the horizon of our destiny.

### καλέω (kaleō) — "to call"
- **Key verses:** Romans 8:30 ("those he predestined, he also CALLED"), Romans 9:11, 1 Corinthians 1:9, 2 Timothy 1:9
- **Theological significance:** In the ordo salutis, calling follows predestination and precedes justification. This is the EFFECTUAL call — not merely an invitation but a summons that accomplishes what it commands.

### ἐνεργέω (energeō) — "to work in, to be effectually at work"
- **Key verse:** Philippians 2:13 — "for it is God who WORKS IN YOU (ὁ ἐνεργῶν ἐν ὑμῖν) both to will and to work for his good pleasure"
- **Theological significance:** God doesn't merely make salvation possible — He actively works in us to produce both the DESIRE (to will) and the ACTION (to work). Same root as English "energy."

### σφραγίζω (sphragizō) — "to seal"
- **Key verses:** Ephesians 1:13, 4:30, 2 Corinthians 1:22
- **Theological significance:** The seal denotes ownership, protection, and authentication. Believers are sealed "for the day of redemption" — the seal cannot be broken.

### θέλημα (thelēma) — "will, desire, purpose"
- **Key verses:** John 1:13, Ephesians 1:5, 9, 11, Romans 9:19
- **Theological significance:** Distinguishing between God's decretive will (what He ordains — always accomplished) and His prescriptive will (what He commands — can be disobeyed) resolves most Arminian objections.

### ἀπόλλυμι (apollymi) — "to destroy, to perish, to lose"
- **Key verses:** John 6:39 ("I should LOSE nothing"), John 10:28 ("they shall never PERISH"), John 17:12
- **Theological significance:** Jesus says He will not LOSE any the Father has given Him. The double negative in John 10:28 (οὐ μὴ ἀπόλωνται) is the strongest possible negation in Greek — "they shall ABSOLUTELY NEVER perish."

## SECTION 3: BEST ARGUMENTS AND ILLUSTRATIONS

### The Doctor and the Corpse (Total Inability)
A doctor walks into a morgue. On the table lies a corpse. Does the doctor wait for the corpse to ask for help? Does he offer medicine and hope the corpse reaches for it? No — the corpse cannot help itself. It is DEAD. If that corpse is ever to live again, the doctor must act first, unilaterally, without the corpse's cooperation. This is the human condition: "dead in trespasses and sins" (Ephesians 2:1). God does not wait for dead sinners to choose life. He gives life to the dead.

### The Adoption Analogy (Unconditional Election)
An orphan sits in an orphanage. She did not choose her adoptive parents — they chose her. They saw her, loved her, and decided to make her their own. She had no say in the matter. She couldn't even understand what adoption meant. The parents' love was the cause, not the child's worthiness. "He predestined us for adoption as sons through Jesus Christ, according to the purpose of HIS will" (Ephesians 1:5).

### The Birth Analogy (Regeneration Precedes Faith)
"You must be born again" (John 3:7). Did you choose to be born the first time? Did you select your parents, your country, your DNA? You had no say whatsoever. Birth is something that happens TO you, not something you decide. The second birth is the same. "Born not of blood nor of the will of the flesh nor of the will of man, but of God" (John 1:13).

### The Lazarus Illustration (Irresistible Grace)
Jesus stood before the tomb and commanded, "Lazarus, come forth!" (John 11:43). Did Lazarus deliberate? Did he weigh his options? Could he have said no? He was DEAD. The command that called him forth was the same command that gave him life. The calling and the quickening were simultaneous. This is effectual calling — God's command creates the very ability to obey it.

### The Prayer Test (Practical Proof)
Do you pray for God to save your loved ones? Then you already believe in sovereign grace. You are asking God to do what only He can do — change a heart. If salvation were merely a matter of human free will, the most logical thing would be to persuade the person, not pray to God. But you pray because you know, deep down, that salvation is God's work.

### The Boasting Test (Ephesians 2:8-9)
If salvation is ultimately decided by your choice, then you have something to boast about. "I was smart enough to accept." "I was humble enough to believe." "I made the right decision." But Paul says salvation is "not of yourselves" and "not of works, so that no one may boast." If YOUR decision is the decisive factor, boasting is not eliminated — it is merely relocated.

### The Chess Grandmaster (Sovereignty and Responsibility)
God is like a chess grandmaster playing against a novice. The grandmaster knows every possible move the novice could make and has planned his strategy accordingly. The novice freely chooses each move — no one forces his hand. Yet the grandmaster's victory was never in doubt. God ordains the end AND the means, and human choices are real yet within His sovereign plan.

### Owen's Trilemma (Limited Atonement)
Christ died for either: (1) all the sins of all people, (2) all the sins of some people, or (3) some sins of all people. If (3), no one is saved because everyone still has some sins unpaid for. If (1), then why isn't everyone saved? The Arminian must say "because of unbelief." But is unbelief a sin? If yes, Christ paid for it, and the unbeliever should be saved. If no, then it's not truly a sin and cannot condemn. Only (2) is logically coherent: Christ died for all the sins of the elect, and they are infallibly saved.

### The Infinite Regress (Against Libertarian Free Will)
If you chose God by your own free will, what caused your will to choose? Another act of will? And what caused THAT? You either have an infinite regress of uncaused causes (logically impossible), or you arrive at a first cause that determined the chain. Reformed theology identifies that first cause: God. "He chose us" (Ephesians 1:4).

### Command ≠ Ability
Jesus said to the man with the withered hand, "Stretch out your hand" (Mark 3:5). The man COULDN'T stretch out his hand — that was the whole problem. The command didn't prove the ability; the power came with the command. Similarly, "Repent and believe" doesn't prove humans can repent and believe on their own. God's commands reveal our DUTY, not our ABILITY.

## SECTION 4: ARMINIAN OBJECTIONS AND RESPONSES

### 1. "Calvinism makes us robots"
**Response:** Not at all. Calvinism teaches that God changes our DESIRES so that we freely choose Him. A robot has no desires — we have new ones. God doesn't override our will; He transforms it. "I will give you a new heart, and a new spirit I will put within you" (Ezekiel 36:26). The regenerated person WANTS God — more freely than ever before.

### 2. "God would be the author of evil"
**Response:** God ordains whatsoever comes to pass (Ephesians 1:11) without being the author of sin. He works through secondary causes. Joseph's brothers freely chose to sell him into slavery — yet God ordained it for good (Genesis 50:20). God is sovereign over evil without being the agent of evil. The Westminster Confession: God "neither is nor can be the author or approver of sin" (V.4).

### 3. "John 3:16 says God loved the WORLD — meaning every individual"
**Response:** "World" (κόσμος) in John's writings has multiple meanings. In John 12:19 the Pharisees say "the WORLD has gone after him" — did every individual follow Jesus? In John 17:9 Jesus says "I am NOT praying for the world." The "world" in John 3:16 means people from every tribe and nation — not Jews only — not every individual without exception.

### 4. "1 Timothy 2:4 says God desires ALL to be saved"
**Response:** The context specifies "kings and all who are in high positions" (v. 2). "All" means all KINDS of people — not all people without exception. If God desired the salvation of every individual with a decretive will, every individual would be saved, for "our God is in the heavens; he does all that he pleases" (Psalm 115:3).

### 5. "2 Peter 3:9 says God is not willing that ANY should perish"
**Response:** Read the full verse: "The Lord is not slow to fulfill his promise as some count slowness, but is patient toward YOU." The "you" is Peter's audience — believers. God is patient toward His elect, not willing that any of THEM should perish but that all of THEM should reach repentance.

### 6. "Acts 7:51 says people RESIST the Holy Spirit"
**Response:** There is a difference between the external call (which can be resisted) and the internal, effectual call (which cannot). Stephen rebuked them for resisting the Spirit's external ministry through the prophets. But when God effectually calls, "those he called, he also justified" (Romans 8:30) — no exceptions, no failures.

### 7. "If predestination is true, why evangelize?"
**Response:** Because God ordains the MEANS as well as the ENDS. "How then will they call on him in whom they have not believed? And how are they to believe in him of whom they have never heard?" (Romans 10:14). Election makes evangelism CERTAIN to succeed — not pointless. Spurgeon: "If God had painted a yellow stripe on the backs of the elect, I would go around lifting shirts. But since He has not, I must preach the gospel to all."

### 8. "Election is unfair"
**Response:** Paul anticipated this objection: "Is there injustice on God's part? By no means!" (Romans 9:14). Fairness would mean everyone goes to hell — ALL have sinned. Election is not unfair; it is MERCIFUL. No one deserves salvation. That God saves ANY is grace. The question is not "Why didn't God choose everyone?" but "Why did God choose ANYONE?"

### 9. "Matthew 23:37 shows God's will can be thwarted"
**Response:** "O Jerusalem, Jerusalem... How often would I have gathered your children together as a hen gathers her brood under her wings, and you were not willing!" Notice: Jesus wanted to gather Jerusalem's CHILDREN, but "you" (the leaders) were not willing. Two different groups. The leaders rejected Jesus; the children (the elect among them) were gathered. God's will was not thwarted.

### 10. "Revelation 3:20 shows Jesus waiting for us to open the door"
**Response:** This verse is written to a CHURCH (Laodicea), not to unbelievers. It is about fellowship, not salvation. Jesus is addressing lukewarm Christians. Using this as a salvation proof text ignores the entire context.

### 11. "What about free will?"
**Response:** Define "free will." If you mean the ability to choose according to your desires — yes, humans have that, and Reformed theology affirms it (compatibilism). If you mean the ability to choose CONTRARY to your nature — no. A dead man cannot choose life. A slave to sin cannot choose righteousness (Romans 6:20). You are free to choose what you WANT — but what you want is determined by your nature, and apart from regeneration, your nature is opposed to God.

## SECTION 5: SECULAR EVIDENCE FOR REFORMED DOCTRINES

### Historical Evidence for Total Depravity
- Every civilization in history has eventually corrupted itself — Roman Empire, Greek democracy, every utopian experiment
- The 20th century: two World Wars, the Holocaust, Stalinist purges, Mao's Cultural Revolution, Cambodian genocide — the "enlightened" modern age produced the worst atrocities in history
- Lord Acton: "Power tends to corrupt, and absolute power corrupts absolutely" — a secular acknowledgment of human corruption

### Psychological Evidence
- The Milgram experiment (1961): 65% of ordinary people administered what they believed were lethal electric shocks when told to by an authority figure
- Stanford Prison Experiment (1971): Normal college students became sadistic guards within days
- Confirmation bias, self-serving bias, and cognitive dissonance research shows humans are systematically incapable of objective self-assessment
- Addiction research: The human will is demonstrably unable to free itself from bondage — confirming "everyone who practices sin is a slave to sin" (John 8:34)

### Philosophical Evidence
- The problem of self-creation: If your will is autonomous, what created your will? (Infinite regress)
- Compatibilism is the dominant position in academic philosophy — most philosophers acknowledge that determinism and moral responsibility are compatible
- Leibniz: "Everything that exists has a sufficient reason for its existence" — including human choices
- Even secular philosophers acknowledge humans don't have libertarian free will (Sam Harris, Daniel Dennett, Derk Pereboom)

### Scientific Evidence
- Neuroscience: The Libet experiments show that brain activity precedes conscious awareness of a decision by ~350ms — your brain "decides" before "you" are aware of deciding
- Behavioral genetics: Studies on identical twins separated at birth show remarkable similarity in personality, preferences, and even religious inclinations — suggesting our "choices" are heavily influenced by factors outside our control
- Evolutionary psychology: If naturalism is true, our desires and choices are the product of blind evolutionary forces — we are not truly "free" in any ultimate sense
