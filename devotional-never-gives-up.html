He Will Never Give Up On You

You have been claimed. You can rest.
5-minute read

There is one thing every human being wants more than anything else on earth, and most of us are terrified to admit it: to be loved by someone who will not leave.

Not the kind of love that stays as long as you are useful. Not the kind that fades when you stop performing. Not the love that checks in on you when things are good and goes quiet when things get ugly. You want — somewhere beneath every defense you have ever built — to be known completely, at your absolute worst, and chosen anyway. You want a love with no exit clause.

You have never found it. Every human love you have ever tasted has had conditions, even the good ones. And so you have learned to perform. To earn. To manage the gap between who you are and who you need to be for someone to stay. That survival instinct followed you straight into your relationship with God — and it has been poisoning it ever since.

Because somewhere along the way, you absorbed a lie so common it feels like oxygen: God's love for you depends on you. On your faithfulness. Your obedience. Your spiritual performance on any given Tuesday. And so you white-knuckle your way through the Christian life, haunted by a question you are afraid to ask out loud: What if I finally go too far?

Scripture answers that question. And the answer will either offend you or set you free.

Claimed Before You Existed

He chose us in him before the foundation of the world, that we should be holy and blameless before him. In love he predestined us for adoption as sons through Jesus Christ, according to the purpose of his will.

Ephesians 1:4–5

Before you were born. Before you sinned. Before you believed. Before the planet you live on was a thought in the mind of anyone but God — He chose you. Not because He looked down the corridor of time and saw that you would be impressive. You would not be. He chose you because He is the kind of God whose love does not need a reason outside itself. His love for you is not a reaction to anything you have done. It is an act of His own sovereign will, rooted in nothing but His own delight.

Do you understand what that means? It means the love of God for you has no cause that you can destroy. You did not start it. You cannot stop it. It was never a response to your goodness, which means your badness cannot undo it. It was decided in eternity — before you had hands to reach for it or a heart to break it.

Did you know? The word "chosen" in Ephesians 1:4 is the Greek word eklego — the same word used when Jesus "chose" the 12 apostles in Luke 6:13. Just as Jesus personally selected His disciples, God personally selected you before the foundation of the world.

Held By Someone Stronger Than You

My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me. I give them eternal life, and they will never perish, and no one will snatch them out of my hand. My Father, who has given them to me, is greater than all, and no one is able to snatch them out of the Father's hand.

John 10:27–29

Two hands. The Son's hand. The Father's hand. You are inside both of them simultaneously, like a seal within a seal. And Jesus does not say "you will probably not perish" or "you will not perish as long as you maintain adequate spiritual performance." He says never. That word is doing all the work in the universe. Your sins cannot overrule it. Your doubts cannot erode it. The worst thing you have ever done — the thing that still wakes you up at night — crashed against that word and did not leave a scratch.

And here is where it gets almost reckless: the security of this promise does not depend on the strength of your grip. It depends on the strength of His. A child in a father's arms may squirm and thrash and try to wriggle free. The child's effort is irrelevant. The father is stronger. And your Father is omnipotent.

This is exactly what Scripture means when it calls you adopted — not a guest in God's house, but a child with an irrevocable inheritance.

The Love That Swallows Every Fear

For I am sure that neither death nor life, nor angels nor rulers, nor things present nor things to come, nor powers, nor height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God in Christ Jesus our Lord.

Romans 8:38–39

Paul is doing something extraordinary here. He is not making a poetic statement. He is conducting a search. He is tearing through every category of reality — time, space, power, the spiritual realm, the material world, the past, the future — hunting for something, anything, that could sever the bond between you and the love of God. And he comes up empty. The list of things capable of separating you from God's love is, and always has been, and always will be: nothing. Nothing in all creation. Which includes you. You are part of creation. And you cannot separate yourself from a love that God Himself has fastened to you.

If we are faithless, he remains faithful — for he cannot deny himself.

2 Timothy 2:13

Nine words that should end every 2 a.m. panic attack you have ever had. Your faith wavers. His does not. Your heart grows cold. His burns with the same intensity it had when He chose you before the stars existed. God's faithfulness is not a mirror of yours — it is rooted in His own nature. He cannot deny Himself. He would have to stop being God to stop loving you. And He will not stop being God.

"He would have to stop being God to stop loving you."

If you've ever wondered whether God's patience has a limit, consider what it means to be sealed by the Holy Spirit — a divine guarantee with no expiration date.

What This Changes

This is not a license to sin. It is the death of the thing that was driving you to sin in the first place: fear. Fear that you are not enough. Fear that God's patience has a limit and you are approaching it. Fear that love is something you earn and therefore something you can lose. That fear has been the engine of your spiritual exhaustion, and the gospel takes a sledgehammer to it.

A slave obeys because he must. A hired worker obeys because he is paid. But a child who has been pulled out of an orphanage and adopted by a father who will never send him back — that child obeys because something has changed inside. Not fear. Not obligation. Love. The most powerful force in the universe is not the threat of punishment. It is the staggering realization that you are loved beyond all reason by Someone who will never stop.

So rest. Not the lazy rest of someone who does not care. The deep, shaking, tearful rest of someone who has been running for years and has finally been told: you can stop now. You were never going to get there on your own. That was never the plan. The plan was that I would carry you.

You are chosen. You are held. You are sealed by the Holy Spirit as God's personal guarantee that the transaction is final. You are loved with a love that was ancient before the world was young. And that love will not let you go. Not today. Not on your worst day. Not ever.

He will never give up on you.

Close your eyes for ten seconds. Ask yourself: what would change — in your prayers, your sleep, your daily breathing — if you believed, all the way to the bedrock of your soul, that the God of the universe has claimed you, and nothing you do can make Him unclaim you? How would you live if the fear of abandonment simply vanished? That is what the gospel offers. Not a probationary love. Not a love that checks your performance reviews. A love that was yours before you were born and will be yours after the stars go dark.
