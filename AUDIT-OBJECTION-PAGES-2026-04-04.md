## CRITICAL WEAKNESSES IDENTIFIED

### CATEGORY 1: "MYSTERY" & "PARADOX" DODGES (Final Answer Uses Paradox)
These pages end objections by appealing to paradox rather than resolving them.

#### **objection-faith-choice.html - Line 669**
**PROBLEM:** Uses Keller quote about paradox as closure to the entire article.
```
"Keller captures the paradox perfectly: your decision is real and it's the gift of God. 
Both are true. The decision you make is yours, but your capacity to make it is His."
```
**ISSUE:** This is a paradox, not a resolution. A smart objector can say: "You just proved you can't explain it. That's exactly my point—this is logically incoherent, so I'll stick with Arminianism." The page should RESOLVE this apparent paradox, not celebrate it.

**STRENGTH:** The article itself has strong arguments before this. But the conclusion weakens it.

---

#### **objection-why-command.html - Line 380**
**PROBLEM:** Treats the decree-command relationship as a paradox to "live in."
```
"The decree doesn't compete with the command. The decree accomplishes itself 
through the command. This is not a paradox for the confused—it is the foundation 
upon which apostolic boldness stands."
```
**ISSUE:** While the statement is true, it's buried in a pull quote rather than being explained. The page ASSERTS the relationship but doesn't demonstrate HOW the decree accomplishes itself through the command. A reader can nod and feel satisfied without truly understanding the resolution.

---

#### **objection-god-could-save-all.html - Line 259**
**PROBLEM:** Tells the reader to "live in the paradox" rather than resolve it.
```
"You don't have to understand how those fit together. You have to obey both. 
Live in the paradox. Preach as if it all depends on you. Pray as if the heavens 
are waiting for your intercession."
```
**ISSUE:** This is pastoral but not apologetically sound. An intelligent objector hears: "I can't actually explain this, so just accept it." A page meant to destroy objections should demolish this one, not ask readers to hold irreconcilables. This FAILS the mandate to "leave zero escape routes."

---

#### **question-was-christ-a-robot.html - Line 322**
**PROBLEM:** Uses "breathtaking paradox" language without full resolution.
```
"Notice the breathtaking paradox: 'I lay it down of my own accord' — that is 
genuine will, genuine agency. And yet: 'This charge I have received from my Father' 
— the will to lay it down was itself given."
```
**ISSUE:** The page calls this a paradox and then uses compatibilism to explain it. But the explanation is thin—it relies on the concept of "two descriptions of the same event from two vantage points" which is itself another way of saying "it's a paradox." The page should dig into WHY these are not contradictory, not just assert they aren't.

### CATEGORY 9: INCOMPLETE RESOLUTION OF THE "RESPONSE" OBJECTION

#### **objection-faith-choice.html - Lines 736-743**
**PROBLEM:** The answer to "isn't faith a response?" is incomplete.
```
"You're right that faith is a response—but a response to what? A response to 
regeneration. The Father reaches down, gives you new life, opens your eyes to 
Christ's beauty. And in that moment of seeing clearly for the first time, you 
respond by believing."
```
**ISSUE:** This says faith is "a response to regeneration," but it doesn't fully explain why "response" doesn't restore human agency. If regeneration happens, and THEN the person freely responds with faith, hasn't the person made a real choice? The page should explain: the regenerated person's response IS regeneration. They're not two sequential events—the regenerated person IS a believer. The response is not an addition to regeneration; it's the expression of it.
