Faith Is a Gift

Ephesians 2:8-9 on one printable page. For anyone who has ever said, "but I chose Him."

Designed to print on one US Letter or A4 page. Use your browser's Print function (Ctrl/Cmd + P).

Print This Page →

1. The Verse

"For it is by grace you have been saved, through faith — and this is not from yourselves, it is the gift of God — not by works, so that no one can boast." — Ephesians 2:8-9. Paul makes two moves. First: faith is the instrument of salvation. Second: that faith is not from you. It is God's gift.

2. The Greek Nails the Door Shut

Some translations blur the argument. The Greek does not. The phrase "this is not from yourselves" uses a neuter pronoun (touto), which does not match the feminine word "faith" (pistis) grammatically — but this is Paul's standard idiom for summarizing an entire clause. The gift is the whole thing: grace, salvation, and the faith that receives it. Paul's next breath confirms it: "not by works, so that no one can boast." The boast being closed off is precisely the boast that your faith was your contribution. Walk the Greek at is faith a gift?.

3. The Question You Cannot Answer

"For who makes you different from anyone else? What do you have that you did not receive? And if you did receive it, why do you boast as though you did not?" — 1 Corinthians 4:7. Paul does not ask this rhetorically. He asks it to close the last door. Every good thing in you — including the faith that saves you — was received. Not earned. Not generated. Received. Begin the walk with where did your faith come from?.

4. The Dead Man Problem

"As for you, you were dead in your transgressions and sins." — Ephesians 2:1. Before grace, you are not sick. You are not weak. You are dead. Dead men do not choose life. They are raised. Jesus did not ask Lazarus if he wanted to come out. Faith is not the dying man pushing back against death; faith is the first breath after the resurrection. Learn the anatomy of the dead-man verb at why we resist.

5. Wanting God Is the Proof

"No one can come to me unless the Father who sent me draws them." — John 6:44. The verb for "draws" is helkyō — to drag, to haul. Every person who ends up at Christ was first dragged. If you find within yourself any true desire to come to Him — even a flicker — that desire is not your contribution. It is the evidence He has already moved. The resistance is proof argument works in reverse: the desire is proof too. You were drawn, not dragged — drawn with cords of love.

6. Paid For, Not Won

"To them it has been granted on behalf of Christ not only to believe in him, but also to suffer for him." — Philippians 1:29. Even belief, Paul says, is granted. A granted thing is not a wage. It is a mercy. The cross paid not only for your sins but for your faith. You bring nothing — not your goodness, not your decision, not your will — you were rescued without a say.

7. The Catch

If faith were your work, it could be lost the moment you faltered. But if it is God's gift, it is kept by the God who gave it. "He who began a good work in you will carry it on to completion until the day of Christ Jesus" (Philippians 1:6). The gift of faith becomes the promise of perseverance. You did not hold Him when you came; you do not hold Him now. He never gives up.
