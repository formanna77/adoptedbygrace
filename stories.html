Stories of Sovereign Grace

He Spoke in Parables

Jesus wrapped infinite truths in finite stories that fishermen could understand and theologians are still unpacking. These stories carry the doctrines of grace in the form He chose: narrative.

"Without a parable He did not speak to them." Matthew 13:34
Story of the Week
A Fable

The Fish Who Chose the Ocean

A small blue fish announces to the reef that he chose the ocean. An old sea turtle asks him a few questions he cannot answer. He was not wrong that he loved the water. He was only wrong about where the loving came from.

Sovereignty John 15:16 Ephesians 1:4-5 10 min read →
Published Stories
An Allegory

The Dead City

An entire city lies under a curse. Every inhabitant is frozen — upright, functioning, convinced they are free. Then a Prince walks through at night, carrying a lantern, and breathes life into those He chooses. They did not know they were dead. That was the nature of the curse.

Election Effectual Calling Ephesians 2:1-5 12 min read →
A Modern Parable

The Surgeon and the Stone Heart

A heart surgeon operates on a patient who never asked for the surgery, couldn't consent to it, and will only discover what happened when she wakes up with a new heart. She didn't choose a new heart. A new heart chose her.

Regeneration Ezekiel 36:26 8 min read →
A Modern Parable

The King's Banquet

A king prepares a magnificent feast and sends servants to invite guests. When they refuse, the king does something unexpected: he commands his servants to compel the poor, blind, and lame to come in. None chose to come. All are welcome at the table.

Irresistible Grace Luke 14:15-24 10 min read →
A Long-Form Allegory

The Orphanage

Every child in the orphanage believed they chose their family on Adoption Day. Then one girl found the records room — and the adoption papers were dated before she was born. She hadn't chosen anyone. Someone had chosen her.

Election Adoption Ephesians 1:4-5 15 min read →
