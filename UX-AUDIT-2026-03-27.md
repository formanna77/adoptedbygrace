# Site Architecture & UX Audit Report — 2026-03-27

## Executive Summary

This session performed a comprehensive audit and overhaul of the information architecture, navigation, and user experience across all 61 pages of adoptedbygrace.net. **Four major improvements were implemented**, resolving the top architectural issues identified in the ROADMAP.

## Improvements Implemented This Session

### 1. Standardized Global Navigation (61 pages)

**Problem:** The site had inconsistent navigation menus across pages. The homepage nav had 7 links (Home, The Evidence, Demolition, Essays, Connections, Freedom, About) while newer pages like resources.html had a different set (Home, Start Here, The Evidence, Systematic, Resources, About). Some pages had duplicate links. **6 hub pages were completely unreachable from the main navigation of older pages.**

**Solution:** Deployed a unified 8-item navigation across all 61 pages:
- Home | Start Here | The Evidence | Systematic | Demolition | Theologians | Resources | About

**Design rationale:** These 8 items represent the complete hub-and-spoke architecture. Standalone pages (Essays, Connections, Freedom) are now accessible through their parent hubs (Resources, The Evidence) rather than consuming top-nav slots. This ensures every major section of the site is one click away from any page.

**Files modified:** All 61 HTML files.

### 2. Standardized Footer Navigation (61 pages)

**Problem:** 4 different footer templates existed. Theologian pages had single-line minimalist footers. Resource pages had resource-specific footers. Navigation links varied between footers. ~15% of pages lacked formal footers.

**Solution:** All 61 pages now share a consistent footer with:
- ADOPTED BY GRACE branding + tagline
- Navigate section with same 8 hub links as top nav
- Copyright + Soli Deo Gloria

### 3. Breadcrumb Navigation (50 pages)

**Problem:** Only 16 pages (19%) had any form of breadcrumb navigation. Users on deep content pages had no way to quickly return to their parent hub.

**Solution:** Added "← Back to [Hub]" breadcrumb navigation to all 50 child pages. Mapping:
- question-* pages → "Back to The Evidence"
- systematic-* pages → "Back to Systematic Theology"
- theologian-* pages → "Back to Theologians"
- demolition-*/objection-* pages → "Back to Demolition"
- resources-*/community → "Back to Resources"
- start-here-phase* → "Back to Start Here"
- story-* → "Back to Stories"
- connections/freedom → "Back to The Evidence"
- essays → "Back to Resources"

### 4. "Continue Exploring" Cross-Links (35 pages)

**Problem:** Content pages operated as dead ends. After reading a doctrine page, users had no guidance on what to read next. Cross-linking was sparse and inconsistent.

**Solution:** Added a "Continue Exploring" section with 4 theologically-related links before the footer on 35 content pages. Every link is hand-curated for doctrinal relevance:
- Question pages link to related doctrines, demolitions, and systematic theology
- Systematic pages link to each other and to relevant Scripture studies
- Demolition pages link to related demolitions and doctrine pages
- Objection pages link to related objections and supporting content
- Theologian pages link to related theologians and their key doctrines

### 5. Additional Fixes

- **Orphaned pages resolved:** objection-why-command.html and objection-author-of-sin.html were unreachable from any hub page. Added an "Objections Answered" section to demolition-hub.html with both live pages + 3 planned.
- **Stories page linked:** stories.html was completely orphaned (only self-referential). Added links from start-here.html and community.html.
- **Homepage "Start Here" CTA:** Added a prominent "Start Here" button alongside "See the Evidence" in the hero section, creating a clear onboarding path for newcomers.
- **Sitemap updated:** Added 3 missing pages (demolition-orthodox-predestination, stories, story-surgeon-stone-heart).
