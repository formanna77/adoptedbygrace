Phase 2 — Foundation

The Five Points of Grace

In 1618, a council of theologians gathered in Dort, Netherlands, to answer five relentless challenges to the biblical doctrine of grace. What emerged was not a human invention, but crystallized truth — five points that Scripture itself has been whispering since the beginning. The council simply distilled what the Bible had always taught.

Before we begin, understand this: TULIP is not the gospel. It is a lens through which to see the gospel in sharper focus. The gospel itself is simple: God saves sinners through Christ. But TULIP answers the how. And the how, once you see it, is breathtaking.

Each petal answers a question. Together, they form a chain of logic so tight, so beautiful, that if you pull one, the whole thing comes undone.

T
Total Depravity
Man's condition: dead, not sick
+

The Question: How bad off are we?

"Total depravity" doesn't mean you're as evil as you could possibly be. It means something far deeper: every part of you is affected by sin. Your mind corrupts truth. Your will rebels. Your affections chase false gods. No part of you escapes. You're not sick in body with a sound mind. You're not wounded but recovering. You're dead — and the dead don't save themselves.

Ephesians 2:1
"And you were dead in the trespasses and sins…"

A dead man cannot negotiate with death. Cannot bargain his way to life. Cannot reach out a hand that no longer moves. The doctor can stand at the tomb all day, prescription in hand, but the dead do not cooperate with medicine. Only resurrection brings the dead to life. And for us — spiritually dead — only God can do that raising.

Romans 3:10–12
"None is righteous, no, not one; no one understands; no one seeks for God. All have turned aside; together they have become worthless; no one does good, not even one."

Why this matters: If you're dead in sin, you didn't decide to get un-dead. God did. Salvation doesn't start with you waking up or reaching out or choosing better. It starts with God moving. Everything else flows from this. Remove this truth and the entire theology of grace collapses.

U
Unconditional Election
God's choice: before the world began
+

The Question: How does God choose who lives?

If everyone is dead in sin, and no one seeks God, then why does anyone wake up? Why does one sinner turn to Christ while another never does? Scripture's answer is scandalous: God chooses. Not based on what He sees in you — not your talent, your faith-to-be, your moral superiority. He doesn't choose you because you're choosing-material. He chooses you because He chooses you. His choice. His grace. His purpose alone.

Ephesians 1:4–5
"He chose us in him before the foundation of the world, that we should be holy and blameless before him. In love he predestined us for adoption to himself as sons through Jesus Christ, according to the purpose of his will."

Read that slowly. He didn't choose us because we would somehow become holy. He chose us in order that we should be holy. The direction matters. His choice comes first. Your holiness — your entire transformation — flows from His election. Holiness is not the reason for your election. Holiness is the fruit of it.

Romans 9:11–13
"Though they were not yet born and had done nothing either good or bad — in order that God's purpose of election might continue, not because of works but because of him who calls — she was told, 'The older will serve the younger.' As it is written, 'Jacob I loved, but Esau I hated.'"

Why this matters: Your salvation wasn't an accident waiting to happen. It wasn't a possibility hinged on your decision. It was written into God's purpose before time began. Before you were born. Before the world was made. If God chose you then, no failure of yours now can undo what was settled then. Your salvation rests on His faithfulness to His own word, not your faithfulness to achieve it.

L
Limited Atonement
Christ's death: purposeful, not hopeful
+

The Question: What did Christ actually do on the cross?

This point gets butchered more than any other. People hear "limited atonement" and think God couldn't save everyone, or didn't want to. Wrong. The question isn't about God's power or love. It's about God's intention. It's about what the cross actually accomplishes. Did Christ merely open a door and hope people walk through? Or did He actually secure something — redemption, not a chance at redemption?

Scripture teaches the latter. Christ didn't make salvation available. He secured it. For His people. Completely. Actually.

John 10:11, 14–15
"I am the good shepherd. The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep. … I know my own and my own know me, just as the Father knows me and I know the Father; and I lay down my life for the sheep."
Matthew 1:21
"She will bear a son, and you shall call his name Jesus, for he will save his people from their sins."

He will save — not "offer to save" or "make it possible for people to maybe save themselves." The verb is simple. Declarative. He — Jesus Christ — will save His people. Not attempt. Will. From their sins. Done. Finished. For everyone the Father gave Him, His death is the complete and final accomplishment of redemption.

Why this matters: If Christ died for you, you're saved. Not eventually, God willing. Saved. His blood covers you. His death is your redemption. The cross isn't a lottery ticket. For those for whom it was intended, it's a guarantee written in His blood.

I
Irresistible Grace
God's call: effectual, not merely offered
+

The Question: Can anyone resist God's call?

"Irresistible grace" is a name that confuses people. You can resist God. People do it every day. But when God purposes to save someone — when He actually calls them into life — that call cannot be thwarted. Not because He drags you unwilling into salvation. But because He changes you so that you don't want to resist anymore. He gives you a new heart. A new desire. You come to Christ freely, gladly, with arms open — because that's what your new heart wants now.

Ezekiel 36:26–27
"And I will give you a new heart, and a new spirit I will put within you. And I will remove the heart of stone from your flesh and give you a heart of flesh. And I will put my Spirit within you, and cause you to walk in my statutes."

Count the verbs. I will give. I will remove. I will put. I will cause. Look at who's doing every single thing. God. All of it. He's not waiting for you to soften your heart. He's pulling out the stone. He's not asking permission to put His Spirit in. He puts it. He doesn't suggest obedience and hope you comply. He causes it. The heart-change? God's work. The obedience? God's work. The faith? God's work.

John 6:37
"All that the Father gives me will come to me, and whoever comes to me I will never cast out."

Why this matters: You're not a Christian because you're smarter than your neighbor who isn't. Not because you had more willpower. Not because you were morally superior and he wasn't. You believe because God made you willing to believe. Your faith is not your achievement. It's His gift. The humility in that truth — the fact that your salvation is entirely God's gracious work and not your accomplishment — is what breaks the pride that keeps men from God in the first place.

P
Perseverance of the Saints
God's keeping: once saved, always kept
+

The Question: Can you lose what God has secured?

The answer is no. But not because your faith is so strong you'll never let go. You will let go. You'll doubt. You'll falter. You'll question. But you won't fall away from salvation — not because you're holding tight to God, but because God is holding tight to you. This doctrine should really be called "the Preservation of the Saints" because the whole point is that God finishes what He started. Your job isn't to white-knuckle your way to heaven. His job is to keep you there.

Romans 8:38–39
"For I am sure that neither death nor life, nor angels nor rulers, nor things present nor things to come, nor powers, nor height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God in Christ Jesus our Lord."

Think about the chain. God chose you before the foundation of the world. He sent His Son to die for you — personally, specifically, you. He sent His Spirit to resurrect you from spiritual death and plant Himself inside you. And you think after all that, He's going to let you slip away at the finish line? That He'll do 99% of the work and then drop you? The same God who moved heaven and earth to save you isn't going to finish the work He started? That's not grace. That's abandonment.

Philippians 1:6
"And I am sure of this, that he who began a good work in you will bring it to completion at the day of Jesus Christ."

Why this matters: You can rest. Not because you're white-knuckling salvation perfectly. Not because you're good enough or faithful enough or smart enough to maintain your salvation. You can rest because your salvation doesn't depend on your performance — it depends on God's character. And God cannot lie. He cannot fail. He will not abandon His own. That's assurance worth having. Real assurance. Unshakable assurance. The kind that lets you sleep at night knowing you're kept by the One who cannot be defeated.

The Logic of Grace

Here's what's beautiful: each point of grace isn't isolated. They're not separate doctrines you can take or leave. They lock together like gears in a machine. Pull one tooth from one gear and the whole mechanism seizes.

You're dead in sin — so dead that salvation cannot begin with you. God must move first. So He chooses you before the world was made. Because He chose you, Christ dies specifically for you — not hopefully, but actually securing your redemption. Because your redemption is secured, the Spirit effectually calls you — not inviting you to make yourself alive, but actually raising you. And because God began this work in eternity and carried it through the cross, He completes it at the end. The whole chain holds. Every link. Forknown. Predestined. Called. Justified. Glorified.

Unbreakable logic. Unstoppable grace.

Romans 8:29–30
"For those whom he foreknew he also predestined to be conformed to the image of his Son, in order that he might be the firstborn among many brothers. And those whom he predestined he also called, and those whom he called he also justified, and those whom he justified he also glorified."

That's the golden chain of redemption. Notice how it works: every name God foreknew, He predestined. Every name He predestined, He called. Every name He called, He justified. Every name He justified, He glorified. The numbers match at every stage. No one drops off in the middle. No one gets lost between predestination and glory. If God knows your name in eternity, He will know your name in heaven. There's no separation, no gap where you could slip through.

Sit With This Truth

  1. Which petal makes your soul sing? Which one makes your mind resist? What's happening in that resistance?
  2. Imagine if even one point of TULIP were false. What would collapse? What would you have to change about how you understand salvation?
  3. Right now — not someday, but today — what would change in your life if you actually believed that God chose you before the world began and will never let you go?
  4. Read Romans 8:29–30 as slowly as you can. What would have to be true about God for Him to lose even one person from that chain?
