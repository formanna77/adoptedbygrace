The Observer Effect of Sin

You cannot see your own blind spot. By definition. And that is exactly why you need an observer from outside.

The Particle That Changes When You Look

In quantum mechanics, observing a particle changes it. An electron unobserved behaves like a wave. But the moment you measure it, the wave collapses. The observation itself alters reality. Now apply this to your soul: when you examine your own will—asking "Am I truly free? Can I really choose God?"—you are using the very faculty whose freedom is in question to conduct the assessment. The instrument of measurement IS the thing being measured.

"The heart is deceitful above all things and beyond cure. Who can understand it? I the Lord search the heart and examine the mind."

Jeremiah 17:9-10 (NIV)

The Trap

If your will is enslaved to sin, the assessment it produces is distorted by the very slavery it is trying to detect. A will in bondage will report that it is free—not because it is lying, but because bondage has corrupted the instrument needed to detect it.

The slave examines his chains and concludes they are jewelry.

You introspect and conclude "I am free to choose God." But the will that produced that conclusion is the will whose freedom is in question. You are the accused, judge, jury, and expert witness in your own trial. And the verdict is always "not guilty." What court in the universe would accept a verdict delivered by the defendant about the defendant?

Gödel's Insight

Gödel proved that any sufficiently complex system contains truths that cannot be proved from within the system itself. No system can fully evaluate itself using only its own resources. The human will is a system—desires, inclinations, habits, biases. The will cannot fully evaluate its own freedom using only its own resources. The truth of its bondage cannot be seen from inside.

You need an external observer. Someone outside the will to assess what the will cannot assess about itself.

The External Observer

"For the word of God is alive and active. Sharper than any double-edged sword, it penetrates even to dividing soul and spirit, joints and marrow; it judges the thoughts and attitudes of the heart."

Hebrews 4:12 (NIV)

The Word is the external observer. It does what your heart cannot do to itself: it judges. And what does it find?

"There is no one righteous, not even one; there is no one who understands; there is no one who seeks God."

Romans 3:10-11 (NIV)

No one. The external observer—the God who stands outside the system—looks at the human will and gives the report the will would never give about itself: You are dead in transgressions and sins. And the dead do not know they are dead.

Why Self-Trust Fails

If the observer effect means your confidence in your own freedom is systematically unreliable, then the claim "I chose God by my own free will" is a self-generated report from a compromised instrument. It comes from the least qualified source. You are asking your will about its own freedom—which is rather like asking a politician whether they're honest. The answer is always yes and always unreliable.

The person who says "I freely chose God" testifies about their will using the very will they're claiming is free. Gödel showed this is impossible. The entire position rests on exactly this unreliable self-report.

But the doctrines of grace ask you to trust the external observer—the God who searches the heart. That observer says: You did not choose Me. I chose you. Your faith was not generated from within. It was given from outside. The Spirit reveals what you could never see.

The Liberation

In quantum mechanics, the observer effect is a constraint on what we can know. In the spiritual realm, the external observer is liberation.

When God looks at you, He does not merely diagnose the bondage. He breaks it. The divine Observer changes the particle. He looks at a dead will and makes it alive. He looks at a system trapped in its own incompleteness and—from outside—provides what the system could never provide for itself.

"I the Lord search the heart" is not a threat. It is a promise.

The God who sees through your self-deception is the God who will not leave you in it. You could not see your own chains. But the One who sees everything saw them for you.

He reached into the system from outside and set you free.
