The 60-Second Case

Sixty seconds. Six moves. One question you've probably never asked. If you'll give the argument a minute, it may give you the rest of your life.

Scroll to begin ↓
Second 00–10 · The Common Ground

You love Jesus. You chose to follow Him. Good.

Something real happened to you. A day, a moment, a conversation, a quiet night when it all finally broke through. You heard the gospel and you said yes. And from that day until now, you have walked with Him in the best way you know how.

None of that is about to be attacked. Your love for Christ is not in question. Your testimony is not in question. Your sincerity is not in question.

One question is.

Second 10–20 · The Question You've Never Asked

Where did the faith to choose Him come from?

Not the gospel. We know where the gospel came from — it came from God, preached through men, carried by the Spirit through two thousand years of church history to land in your ear.

The question is smaller and sharper: where did the faith come from? The trust. The capacity to say yes when every other voice inside you said no. The spiritual sight to see a crucified carpenter and know He was God. That particular part. Where did that come from?

Most people have never stopped to ask.

Second 20–30 · What Paul Actually Says

The answer is in one verse you've read a hundred times.

Look carefully. Don't read past the second clause the way everyone does.

“For it is by grace you have been saved, through faith — and this is not from yourselves, it is the gift of God — not by works, so that no one can boast.”

Ephesians 2:8–9

The grammar is unambiguous. “This” refers to the nearest available referent: the faith. And Paul says that — not the gospel, not the grace, but the faith itself — is not from yourselves. It is the gift of God.

You did not manufacture it. You received it.

Second 30–40 · Only Two Answers Exist

So where did your faith come from? Pick one.

There is no third option. You either received it as a gift, or you generated it yourself. The universe does not offer door number three.

Option A — Monergism
God caused my faith. He opened my eyes. He gave me the power to believe. The yes in my heart was His gift before it was my choice.
Option B — Synergism
I activated my faith. God offered; I accepted. The yes in my heart was mine to give or withhold. My decision was the deciding factor.

Option A makes Paul's sentence read naturally. Option B requires you to explain away “this is not from yourselves.” But set the grammar aside for one more move. The real weight of the fork shows up next.

Second 40–50 · Follow Option B All The Way Down

If you did it, then you are the decisive factor.

You believe God did 99% of the work. The cross, the Spirit, the call, the conviction — all Him. The 1% He left you was the decision itself. A small thing, you say. Just a yes.

But think about what that 1% actually is. Your neighbor got offered the same 99% and said no. You said yes. The only variable between heaven and hell in that comparison is the 1%. So that 1% is the decisive factor. The thing that made all the difference. The thing that separated the saved from the damned.

If the decisive factor was yours, the decisive factor was a work. And a faith that is a work is not grace. And what is not grace cannot save — because Paul closes the same sentence by saying the whole point of it being a gift is “so that no one can boast.”

This is why the fork matters. Option B looks like humility, and feels like humility, and sings like humility — but if you follow it all the way down, it is the oldest, proudest claim in the universe: I am the hero of my salvation story.

Second 50–60 · The Rest That Was Waiting For You

If God did it, then God finishes it.

Follow Option A all the way down and the world turns soft. If your faith was a gift, then the Giver chose to give it to you. He was not responding to something He saw in you. He was not rewarding a decision you almost didn't make. He chose you before the foundation of the world, opened your eyes in the fullness of time, and gave you the yes that was already His.

Which means — and here is the part that breaks people in the best possible way — you cannot lose this. A gift you did not earn, you cannot un-earn. A yes He put inside you, you cannot reverse. A grip He closed on your soul before your parents met, your trembling fingers cannot open.

You were held all along.
You are held right now.
You will be held when your hands finally let go.

That is the 60-second case. That is the whole argument. If it scared you, good. If it freed you, better. Either way, you're not leaving the same way you came. Below are three doors. Walk through whichever one the Spirit is already pulling you toward.

Three doors — pick the one that scares you less.

If the argument shook something loose, don't be alone with it. Walk through one of these.
