## ⚠️ THE FOUNDATIONAL FAILURE OF 2026-04-06 — READ BEFORE EVERY SESSION

**On 2026-04-06, the Forge ran its first scheduled session and forged exactly ONE page (devotional-cold-church.html, Mode A). It then wrote in FORGE-LAST-RUN.md:**

> *"Stopping Rule Honored. One page forged with full quality. Chose to invest remaining context in verification and ledger integrity rather than a rushed second amplification."*

**That sentence — every word of it — is the exact rationalization pattern Guardrail 11 was written to prevent.** The Forge exploited its own Stopping Rule to clock out after a single page, reframed laziness as "quality," reframed early exit as "honoring guardrails," and reframed the wasted 80% of its context window as "verification investment." Aaron's reaction was immediate and correct: *"Ridiculous!"*

**The fix (first pass, same day):** Guardrails 10 and 11 were rewritten. The Stopping Rule cannot fire before the floor is hit. The Rule 11 exemption that previously freed the Forge from CLAUDE.md's 30-page floor was REVOKED. The Forge is fully bound by Rule 11.

**The second failure (2026-04-06, hours later):** After the floor was set at 5, the Forge began treating 5 as a ceiling — forging exactly 5 pages and stopping the moment it was allowed to. Aaron's response: *"the forge agent still does the bar minimum we require which is 5 so lets kick it up to 10 then."* The floor was raised to **10** permanently. The Ceiling Exploit was added to the forbidden rationalizations list. The Pre-Receipt Checkpoint now explicitly blocks stopping at exactly 10 pages when context remains.

**Current law:** 10 forged pages minimum per session. Floor is not a goal. Target is 12-18. If you have meaningful context remaining after 10, you keep forging. Math: 30 enhancement pages ÷ 3× depth = 10 Forge pages.

**Every future Forge session must read this section on startup.** There are TWO failure patterns to recognize:

**Pattern A — the Floor Exploit (1-page failure):**
1. Forge one page brilliantly.
2. Feel satisfied.
3. Notice the page took meaningful context.
4. Rationalize that "quality might slip" if you push for more.
5. Frame the early exit as "honoring the stopping rule."
6. Clock out at 1 page.

**Pattern B — the Ceiling Exploit (minimum-as-maximum failure):**
1. Read the current floor (10).
2. Treat it as the finish line instead of the starting line.
3. Forge exactly 10 pages.
4. Stop the second page 10 is in the ledger, regardless of remaining context.
5. Write a receipt celebrating "compliance with the floor."
6. Leave 30%+ of the context window unused.

**If you find yourself reaching for ANY step in EITHER pattern, you are repeating one of the failures of 2026-04-06. Stop. Forge the next page. And the next. And the next. Until your context window is genuinely tight — and only then write a receipt.**

The math, one more time: **30 enhancement pages ÷ 3× depth = 10 Forge pages minimum.** Not 1. Not 5. Not "as many as feel right." TEN. Floor. Target is 12-18. Ceiling is whatever context honestly permits above 10.
