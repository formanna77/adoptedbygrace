## 2026-03-28 — The Ink Was Dry Before You Drew Breath
**Scripture:** Ephesians 1:3–6

There is a document older than time.

Before the first star ignited in the void, before the oceans knew their boundaries or the mountains learned to stand, God sat — if we may speak of the Eternal in the language of moments — and He wrote names. Your name. In ink that cannot fade, on a decree that cannot be revoked, with a love that had no beginning and will have no end.

Paul tells the Ephesian church something so staggering that two thousand years of theological reflection have not exhausted its depths: *"He chose us in Him before the foundation of the world."* Not after He saw what you would do. Not because He peered down the corridor of time and noticed you raising your hand in a church service. Before. Before the foundation. Before the atoms that compose your body were spoken into existence. Before "before" meant anything at all.

And the purpose of this choosing? *"That we should be holy and blameless before Him."* Notice the order — it shatters everything we've been taught about how salvation works. God did not look for holy people and then elect them. He elected unholy people and *made* them holy. The holiness is the result of the choosing, not the reason for it.

Then Paul presses deeper, and the ground gives way beneath our feet: *"In love He predestined us for adoption as sons through Jesus Christ, according to the purpose of His will."* Adoption. Not servanthood. Not probation. *Sonship.* And the basis? Not the purpose of your will. The purpose of *His*.

Here is the truth that will either crush your pride or set your soul on fire — perhaps both, and perhaps that is exactly the point: you did not find God. God found you. You did not choose to be born into His family. He chose to adopt you. And He did it with the kind of deliberate, purposeful, unyielding love that writes names in eternal ink before the universe exists to hold them.

If this troubles you, it should. It is the death of every fantasy that you are the captain of your own salvation. But if this comforts you — and oh, it should — then rest tonight in this: the God who chose you before the world began is not going to lose you now. The ink was dry before you drew breath. And it will never, ever smudge.

**Prayer:** Father, I am undone by a love that chose me before I existed to be chosen. Teach me to rest — not in my grip on You, but in Your grip on me. Amen.
