Philosophy of Truth

Where Reason Meets Revelation

Deep philosophical expeditions into why the truth of sovereign grace isn't just biblical — it's inescapable. Follow the logic of love, the structure of reality, and the architecture of the human heart to their unavoidable conclusion.

Philosophy Didn't Discover Grace — Grace Explains Philosophy

Every great philosophical question — about freedom, love, justice, identity, truth — runs headlong into the same wall. The wall is you. Not your intellect. Your will. The human mind is brilliant at constructing arguments. It is terrible at admitting it needs rescue.

This section doesn't ask you to accept what Scripture says on authority alone (though it should be enough). Instead, it walks you through reality — marriage, love, merit, self-knowledge, resistance — and asks one simple question at each stop: What must be true for this to make sense?

The answer, every single time, points in the same direction. And that direction terrifies the autonomous self — because it leads to a God who chose first.

"The heart is deceitful above all things, and desperately sick; who can understand it?"

Jeremiah 17:9

Philosophy cannot save you. But it can strip away the last hiding place of a will that refuses to admit it has been found.

Why Philosophy Matters for the Soul

These aren't academic exercises. Every philosophical inquiry on this page is aimed at the same target: the hiding place of the autonomous self. Some people will never be reached by a Bible verse alone — not because Scripture lacks power, but because they've built a fortress of reason around their resistance. These pages enter through the gate of reason and open it from the inside.

For the Thinker Who Can't Surrender

Some minds need the logic laid bare before the heart will follow. These pages refuse to skip steps or hide behind appeals to mystery.

For the Believer Who Can't Explain

You know these truths in your bones but struggle to articulate why. These pages give you the vocabulary and the arguments.

For the Skeptic Who Thinks Grace Is Arbitrary

The most common objection is that election is "random" or "unfair." Philosophy reveals it is the most rational, the most loving, the most beautiful reality conceivable.

For the Resistant Who Don't Know Why

You feel the hostility but you've never examined it. These pages are mirrors. They show you what's behind the anger — and it isn't what you think.
